02 · DEFINE

Turn business outcomes into assessable learner behaviours

Backward design · Bloom's taxonomy · competency mapping · action mapping. The translation layer between business and content.

Frameworks I apply here

Backward design as the starting point

I begin every project from "what should the learner be able to do on day one" — then build measurable assessment evidence before designing learning experiences. Bloom verbs calibrate exercise format; Action Mapping strips content that doesn't connect to a behaviour.

06 · MEASURE

Beyond happy sheets — measure behaviour and business impact

Kirkpatrick L1–L4 · learning analytics · NPS · drop-off analysis · A / B testing. Where most L&D stops at L1 and where I push for L3+.

Frameworks I apply here

Kirkpatrick all four levels, designed in

L4 (business results) is decided first — every other measurement instrument hangs off it. L2 (learning) goes into the LMS as quiz / interaction analytics. L3 (behaviour) needs a stakeholder rubric and on-the-job evidence — the hardest level, and the one that separates training from theatre.

Kirkpatrick L1–L4 Learning analytics NPS Drop-off analysis A / B testing

Real example from my work

Algonova · video-lesson analysis pipeline

Built 25+ Python scripts (~4 700 LOC): Whisper transcription of recorded lessons, video frame extraction, Google Sheets / Drive aggregation, and Excel pedagogical scoring reports. Used to iterate on team-wide lesson quality based on a 500 MB dataset — L2 lesson-quality and L3 instructor behaviour evidence, automated.

07 · ITERATE

Treat the course like a product — version, refresh, kill what doesn't work

Continuous improvement · A / B testing · content refresh cycles · performance support layering.

Frameworks I apply here

Continuous improvement, owned

Content rots. Products change. The course version that shipped 6 months ago is usually wrong somewhere. I run iteration as a scheduled loop with a refresh calendar and a kill list, not as ad-hoc reaction to complaints. 5 Moments of Need (Mosher / Gottfredson) reminds me that some learner needs belong in performance support, not in re-training.

Continuous improvement A / B testing Content refresh cycles 5 Moments of Need

Localized 15+ B2B courses for the Latin American market, adapting content to cultural and professional context. Designed and implemented a cross-functional status tracking system that improved team visibility and reduced handoff delays — iteration discipline at the team level, not just per-course.
