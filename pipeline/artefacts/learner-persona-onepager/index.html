03

Three real scenarios

where the persona uses what we ship
Scenario A · happy path
8:45 Monday — pulls today's call list from Salesforce, scans Gong snippets of last week's top 3 demo openers, drafts 12 personalised opening lines in 25 minutes, starts dialling at 9:30. By 11:00 has booked 2 discovery calls. Uses the program's "trigger phrase → prop" cheat sheet on screen during call 11.
Scenario B · pressure path
Late Wednesday — 3 days behind monthly meeting quota. Sales floor is loud. Manager has pinged Slack twice. Persona starts cutting corners on personalisation; uses the program's "if you only have 15 min" express-flow card to triage which 20 accounts to call hot.
Scenario C · stuck path
First call with a Series-B fintech CFO who interrupts after 8 seconds: "We already use Brex." Persona freezes — script doesn't cover competitive displacement. Opens the program's objection matrix on a second monitor, finds the "additive-not-switch" framing, recovers the call. Books a 20-min follow-up.
04

Top 3 pains

what costs them sleep
  • Cold-open rejection inside 10 seconds, ~70 % of dials.
  • Re-keying notes between Salesforce + Outreach + manager-asked-for spreadsheet.
  • No clean framework for what to do when the buyer says "we already use X" — wastes the rest of the call.
05

Top 3 gains

what would make their week
  • Predictable 90-second window after the opener.
  • A pasteable objection response that doesn't sound like marketing copy.
  • A leaderboard rank they can show their partner / parents without explanation.
06

Anti-patterns · what to NOT design for this persona

named misreads we will not repeat
Anti-pattern A: long-form async video. They do not have 14 uninterrupted minutes mid-shift; they have 90-second gaps between calls. Microlearning ≤2 min or it goes unwatched.
Anti-pattern B: tool-agnostic theory ("understand your buyer"). They need named-tool, named-objection, named-response cards. Theory without the worked example reads as L&D speak and gets ignored.
Anti-pattern C: gamification with weekly leaderboards visible to everyone. The bottom-third quits applying the framework once they realise public ranking is locked in. Use private % completion + manager 1:1 instead.
