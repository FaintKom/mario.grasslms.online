Stakeholder Interview Script

This is the script I run in 30–45-minute kickoff calls with a learning sponsor. Robinson 4-needs ordering; Mager root-cause guard inside the performance section; scope discipline closes the call. Designed to run from memory after one read-through.

Duration: 30–45 min Setting: Zoom / Meet / in-person Output: 9-section Needs Assessment + go / no-go
Opening 3 min · Business need 7 min · Performance gap + root cause 10 min · Audience + environment 7 min · Constraints + scope 5 min · Wrap + next step 3 min
You say Listen for If you hear X, dig into Y
01

Opening · frame the call

3 min · permission + scope
You say
"Thanks for the time. The goal of this call is for me to understand what you actually need from this program — not to pitch a solution. I'll ask a series of questions about your business, your team's behaviour today, and the constraints we'll work inside. By the end we'll know whether training is the right intervention at all, and if it is, what it has to do."
You say
"Three ground rules. One — I'll repeat back what I hear so we agree before we move on. Two — I'd rather hear a brutal answer than a polite one. Three — if a question doesn't apply, just say so and we skip it. Sound fair?"
Listen for
Energy + speed of response. A sponsor who answers in two sentences is engaged. A sponsor who deflects to "ask my team" is not the real sponsor — surface that and reschedule.
02

Business need · Robinson #1

7 min · "what changes for the business if this works?"
You say
"In one sentence — if this program ships and lands, what changes for the business in six months?"
You say
"Which existing KPI moves? Pick one — revenue, ramp time, churn, NPS, defect rate, anything you already report on. We need a number that's already on a dashboard somewhere."
You say
"What's the baseline number right now, and what would success look like in three / six / twelve months?"
If you hear
"We just need everyone trained on X." → Dig: "What problem is the lack of training causing today? Walk me through the most recent example you saw." — they often skip the business outcome and jump to the solution.
If you hear
"It's hard to put a number on it." → Dig: "What would your CFO ask if this line item showed up in next quarter's budget?" — forces specificity by proxy.
03

Performance gap · Robinson #2 + Mager root-cause

10 min · current behaviour, desired behaviour, why the gap
You say
"Describe what your top performers do that the rest don't. Not the abstract competency — the actual moves. What do they say, click, choose, prioritise?"
You say
"Now the same question for the bottom 25 %. What do they do that the top performers don't?"
You say
"If a learner finishes this program, what is the first observable thing they do differently on the job on Monday morning?"
You say
"Mager's root-cause check. Imagine I drop a perfectly trained employee into the role tomorrow. Would they hit the target — or would the process, tooling, incentives, or manager get in the way? Walk me through the top 3 non-training causes you can think of."
If you hear
"We've trained them before and nothing stuck." → Dig: "What changed after the last training? Did the process, the tool, the manager's behaviour, or the metric they're measured on change? If nothing changed, that's a process problem, not a training problem."
If you hear
"The CRM / tool is bad but we can't change it." → Flag now: "Then training will lift the ceiling by maybe 10–15 %. The remaining 85 % needs a tooling fix and is out of our scope. Should I document that in the proposal?"
Listen for
Whether the sponsor blames learners or system. Sponsors who blame learners often have a Mager problem — the system is broken and training is being asked to compensate. Name it on the call.
04

Audience + environment · Robinson #3 + #4

7 min · who learns, in what setting
You say
"Walk me through the audience. Role, tenure, prior knowledge, what they're already good at, what they hate doing."
You say
"How much time per learner can we realistically take — in total, and in any single sitting? Be honest. If it's 'whatever it takes' that's actually 30 minutes a week."
You say
"What's already in place that we'll inherit or work around? LMS, authoring tools, knowledge base, internal comms channels, manager 1:1 cadence?"
You say
"Any compliance, accessibility, or DEI requirements I should know about up front? (WCAG, GDPR, regulated industry, multilingual workforce.)"
If you hear
"They're all remote, all senior, all different time zones." → Lean async + microlearning + manager-led debrief. Live cohort sessions become extraction-of-value, not delivery-of-content.
05

Constraints + budget + dates

5 min · stop scope creep early
You say
"What's the budget bracket — even ballpark? <€5K, €5–25K, €25–75K, >€75K. I don't need exact, I need the order of magnitude so I don't over-design."
You say
"Hard launch date or soft target? What happens externally if we slip a week, a month?"
You say
"Who else has a vote on what ships? SME, manager line, legal / compliance, brand, IT? Name them so I can include them in review cycles up front, not at the gate."
06

Out of scope · what we are NOT solving

3 min · Malamed scope discipline
You say
"I want to name three things we are explicitly not solving in this engagement. Things that came up but live outside the brief. Helps us defend the timeline and gives you a clean list for a future engagement. What would you put on that list?"
Listen for
Sponsor's willingness to scope down. A sponsor who can't name a single out-of-scope item will let scope creep onto the build. Name something yourself if they go blank — it sets the precedent.
07

Wrap · next step + commitment

3 min · close clean, propose async confirm
You say
"Two things to close. One — I'll send you a one-page Needs Assessment within 48 hours that captures everything we just covered. Two — I'll propose three options at three price points so you can choose how much program you want to buy. Sound right?"
You say
"Last thing — name one person on your side who'll be my single point of contact for SME interviews and review cycles. I need one throat to choke, not a committee."

09

SE adaptation · script sections → MEDDIC letters

when the sponsor is a Sales Enablement / RevOps lead

The base script works for any learning-program kickoff. When the sponsor sits inside a Revenue org, the same questions map cleanly onto MEDDIC qualification — useful for L&D / Enablement / RevOps audiences who already speak that vocabulary.

Script sectionMEDDIC letterWhat surfaces
§02 Business needM · MetricsWhich KPI moves; baseline + target.
§05 Constraints + dates + votersE · Economic buyerReal budget holder + who else has a vote.
§02 + §03 + §06D · Decision criteriaWhat "success" looks like + what is explicitly out of scope.
§05 Constraints + §07 WrapD · Decision processReview cycles, sign-off path, named SPOC.
§03 Performance gap + Mager guardI · Identify painObservable behavioural gap + non-training root causes.
§07 SPOC askC · ChampionSingle point of contact on the sponsor's side.
§01 Opening + §05 votersC · CompetitionWhat else has been tried; who else is being evaluated.

The MEDDIC map is not a re-script — it's a translation aid so the Enablement / RevOps sponsor can recognise their own qualification discipline inside an ID kickoff.
