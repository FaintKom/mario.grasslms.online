Sales Enablement · Facilitator Guide

Sales Kickoff —
FinTech B2B Card Onboarding

A 90-minute facilitator-led workshop that turns the self-paced SCORM module into rehearsed muscle. Five blocks, trio role-play, observer rubric, and a commitment-card bridge from workshop to first 5 live calls.

Duration
90 minutes
Cohort size
8–14 reps
Audience
SDR / AE — post-SCORM module
Facilitator
Enablement lead, senior AE, or coach
Delivery
In-room or virtual (Zoom / Meet)
Companion artefact
SCORM microlearning (mandatory pre-work)
Tools
Print-ready HTML / PDF · Articulate Rise (equiv.)

What this guide is

A complete facilitator script for one cohort run, with every block scoped to a learning outcome and tied to a measurement instrument. Not a slide deck — a working document. Print double-sided, walk the room with it, or screen-share the URL.

How to use it

  1. Read pages 2–3 (pre-work + agenda overview) at least 24 h before the session.
  2. Print pages 8 + 9 (commitment card + observer rubric) — one of each per rep.
  3. Run the workshop from pages 4–7. Each page covers one block; sidebars cover in-room vs virtual.
  4. Use page 10 (post-workshop checklist) within 24 h of session close.

Before the workshop

The session is 90 minutes. Half of its success is decided in the 24 hours before it starts.

48 hours before

  • Confirm all reps have completed the SCORM microlearning. Pull completion + quiz-score report from the LMS.
  • Identify any rep with quiz score < 80 % — schedule a 15-min 1:1 with their manager *before* the workshop, not during.
  • If virtual: send Zoom / Meet link + ICS calendar invite with agenda summary embedded.

24 hours before

  • Print: 1× commitment card per rep, 1× observer rubric per rep. Both at the back of this guide.
  • If in-room: confirm room with movable seating (no fixed lecture rows). Markers and sticky notes per table.
  • If virtual: pre-create 3–5 Zoom breakout rooms (one per trio).

30 minutes before

  • Open the SCORM module on your screen — you'll reference Screens 4 + 5 in Block 2.
  • Open the manager-rubric Slack template (appendix) ready to paste post-workshop.
  • Tea, water, head-clear.

Block 1 · Set + check-in

Calibrate energy. Surface the room's current confidence level. Make it psychologically safe to admit a call went badly.

Andragogy · safety Knowles · need-to-know

Materials

  • None on table. Maximum eye contact.

00:00 — Open

Smile. State the workshop premise in one sentence. Skip the company-update preamble; reps know it.

In 90 minutes we are not going to learn about the card. You already learned about the card in the SCORM module. We are going to drill the moves you will use on your next 5 calls — out loud, with each other, so the first time you hear yourself say them is here, not in front of a real buyer.

00:01 — Check-in (round-the-room, ≤30 s each)

Two questions, in order. Speed matters more than depth.

One: in the last 7 days, what is one moment in a call where you felt off-balance? One sentence. Two: on a scale of 1 to 5, how confident are you walking into your next SMB call right now?

Capture the average confidence number on flipchart / whiteboard. You will revisit it in Block 5.

Cohort >10 reps? Pair-first variant: 1 min in pairs trading the same 2 questions, then sample 4 voices in main room (2 min). Keeps Block 1 within 5–8 min.

In-room

  • Stand in a loose horseshoe, not seated. Standing keeps it under 5 min.
  • Write the confidence average big on the flipchart.

Virtual

  • Cameras on for this block — no exception. State it in the meeting invite.
  • Use a Zoom poll for the 1–5 confidence rating; screenshot the result.

Common pitfall

  • Letting one rep tell a 3-minute war story. Cut firmly — "we will come back to this in Block 3, hold it". Whole block is 5 min total.

Block 2 · Drill positioning openers

Move the four trigger → opener pairs from the SCORM screen into rep muscle memory. Out loud, with a partner.

4C/ID · part-task practice Worked-example fading Bowman · talking > listening

Materials

  • Talk-track handout (printed double-sided, one per rep) — or use SCORM Screen 4 on screen.
  • Timer (5-min rounds).

00:05 — Frame (≤90 s)

In the SCORM module you saw the four trigger phrases and the four openers. You read them. Today you say them. Pair up. One of you is the buyer, one is the rep. The buyer picks a trigger from the card and says it in their own words — not word for word. The rep fires back the matching opener. Out loud, full sentence. Then switch.

00:06 — Round 1: openers (5 min)

Pairs drill. Walk the room (or breakout rooms) listening for two things:

  1. Are reps using the prop name (e.g. "real-time spend control") or paraphrasing into something vague?
  2. Are they following the opener with a question, or finishing flat?

00:11 — Round 2: pivot (5 min)

Same pairs swap roles. New rule: the buyer adds one piece of context after the trigger ("we already have a Visa from our bank, my husband uses it for the warehouse"). The rep must hear it and acknowledge before pivoting to the opener.

If you don't hear them say something that names what the buyer just said, stop them gently. We are training the listen-then-pivot move, not the recite-the-opener move.

00:14 — Debrief (1 min)

One question to the room. Don't take a tour; one or two voices only.

What did your partner do that you want to steal?

In-room

  • Stand-up pairs, scattered around the room. Sitting kills energy.
  • You walk; don't observe from the front.

Virtual

  • Zoom breakout rooms of 2. 5-min rounds, auto-return.
  • Hop between rooms; spend ≤1 min in each. Listen for the acknowledge move.

Common pitfall

  • Pairs go into long meta-conversation about the product instead of practising. Cue them back: "say the opener out loud, then switch".

Block 3 · Trio role-play · Round 1

First whole-task practice. Three roles: rep, María (the buyer), observer with rubric. The observer is the most important role.

4C/ID · learning task Kolb · concrete experience Schön · reflection-in-action

Materials

  • Observer rubric (printed, one per rep — see page 09).
  • Scenario cards (3 scenarios, each in an envelope — see appendix).
  • Timer.

00:20 — Form trios (2 min)

Mix tenure: each trio gets one rep with >3 live calls, one with 0–2, one in the middle if available. Mixed-experience trios outperform same-tenure ones in our pilot data.

00:22 — Introduce the observer role (3 min)

Hand out the observer rubric. Walk them through it once on the board.

The observer is not a passive watcher. You hold the rubric. After the role-play, you give two pieces of feedback to the rep: one specific move that worked, one specific move you would change. Not "good job". Specific. We are training each other to give feedback as well as to receive it.

00:25 — Round 1 role-play (15 min)

Each trio runs the scenario from envelope A. 8 min role-play + 4 min observer-led debrief + 3 min rotate.

Walk the room (or hop breakouts). Listen for one thing per trio — don't try to listen to everything.

What to listen for · facilitator note

  • Did the rep acknowledge what the buyer said before pivoting to the opener? (Re-state the objection back.)
  • Did the rep finish with a question, or talk past the close?
  • Did the observer give specific feedback, not vague encouragement?

Common pitfall · senior-rep resistance

  • Senior AEs may refuse role-play ("I close 7-figure deals, I'm not doing trios with a junior SDR"). Frame their participation as coaching the next cohort, not being coached. Pair them as the observer in round 1 so they evaluate technique without being on the spot. Round 2 they can opt in to the rep role with a willing partner.

In-room

  • Trios spread out — give each a corner or table. Distance reduces overhearing self-consciousness.

Virtual

  • Pre-create breakouts of 3. Push scenario card via chat at start.
  • Hop trio rooms; spend ≤2 min per room. You will miss things — that's OK; the observer rubric catches them.

Block 4 · Round 2 + framework debrief

Second round with a harder scenario, role rotation. Then name the pattern: the move is acknowledge → opener → question, not opener → opener → opener.

Kolb · reflective + abstract Shank · 1-positive / 1-specific

00:45 — Rotate trios (2 min)

Roles rotate — Round 1's rep is Round 2's observer. The harder scenario (envelope B — a buyer who has been pitched 3 competing cards already) goes to the trios who handled A best.

00:47 — Round 2 role-play (18 min)

10 min role-play + 5 min observer-led debrief + 3 min trio synthesis ("what's the one thing all three of us agree the rep did well").

01:05 — Plenary debrief: name the rule (8 min)

Reps come back to the main room. Don't lecture. Pull the rule out of them.

From what you just saw across two rounds — what's the move? When the buyer says something and the rep just fires the opener, what's missing?

Wait. Let it land. If no one says acknowledge, prompt: "what did the best reps do before they delivered the opener?". Eventually you will hear "they repeated it back" or "they showed they heard it". That is the rule.

Right. The move is acknowledge → opener → question. Not opener-opener-opener. The 1-second acknowledge buys you the right to deliver the substance. That is the whole workshop in 8 words.

This 3-step pattern draws on Sandler upfront-contract discipline + Challenger commercial-teaching framing. We are not the first to name it — we are putting it on muscle memory.

Cite the framework explicitly

Per Mayer's multimedia principles — don't double up. One trigger heard → one prop demoed → one next-step ask. The acknowledge is the signalling step that tells the buyer their words registered. Without it, the opener sounds rehearsed.

Block 5 · Commitment cards + wrap

Turn workshop L2 evidence into L3 behaviour. Without this block, the workshop is theatre.

Kolb · active experimentation Performance consulting · L3 bridge Thalheimer · performance L1

Materials

  • Commitment cards (printed, one per rep — page 08).
  • Pens.
  • Optional: Mentimeter / Zoom poll for the L1 micro-pulse.

01:15 — Hand out commitment cards (1 min)

Three sentences. Three moves you will try on your next three calls. Not "I will be more confident". Specific. "On my next call, when the buyer says X, I will Y." Names, phrases, the literal acknowledge sentence. Write it.

01:16 — Silent write (5 min)

Total silence. No music. Reps write. You stay in front, don't loom over shoulders.

01:21 — Pair share (4 min)

Reps pair (different partner from Block 2 if possible). Read each other their three commitments out loud. Partner just listens — no feedback. The point is that someone heard you say it. Public commitment outperforms private.

01:25 — Photograph commitment cards (2 min)

Reps photograph their own card. Photo goes to their manager within 24 h — manager opens the 1:1 next week with the three commitments. This is the L3 bridge. The card itself stays with the rep on their desk.

01:27 — Confidence re-check + L1 micro-pulse (3 min)

Anonymous. Per Thalheimer, three predictive questions:

  1. "I could use what I learned today on a real call this week." (1–5)
  2. "My confidence walking into my next SMB call is..." (1–5)
  3. One sentence: "the most useful 10 minutes of this workshop was..." (free text)

Use Mentimeter / Zoom poll / sticky on the wall (anonymous). Capture the average vs the Block 1 baseline number. If delta < +0.5, the workshop didn't move the needle and the design needs iteration.

Close

You came in at [Block 1 average] confidence. You're leaving at [Block 5 average]. The card on your desk is the bridge. See you in a week — I'll be reviewing one of your calls. Thank you.

Observer rubric

For role-play in Blocks 3 + 4

Score 1 (not yet) → 5 (strong). You are not grading the rep — you are noticing for them. After the role-play, give one specific positive observation and one specific improvement. Avoid "good job".

Dimension What it looks like 1 2 3 4 5
Acknowledge Repeats / re-states the buyer's words before pivoting to the opener. ☐☐☐☐☐
Trigger → prop match Picks the right value prop for the buyer's pain phrase (not a feature dump). ☐☐☐☐☐
Opener fluency Delivers the opener naturally — not robotic, not vague. ☐☐☐☐☐
Ends with a question Finishes the turn with an open question, not a statement that closes the door. ☐☐☐☐☐
Listens after Lets silence sit — does not fill the gap with another sentence. ☐☐☐☐☐
Advanced the deal Ended the conversation with a clear next step + named owner + date locked. ☐☐☐☐☐

After the workshop

L3 evidence is decided in the 14 days after the workshop. This page is the L3 instrument.

Within 24 hours

  • Email cohort: link to commitment card PDF; reminder to send their card photo to their manager.
  • Slack each rep's manager with the rep's three commitments — copy-paste template below.
  • Log L1 average vs Block 1 baseline; note delta in cohort-tracking sheet.

Within 7 days

  • Cohort spaced-retrieval mini-quiz (3 Q, ≤3 min) — auto-sent by LMS.
  • Manager confirms 1:1 happened with commitment-card review.

Within 14 days

  • Pull 1 call recording per rep. Score against the same observer rubric (page 09). Target mean ≥ 3.5/5.
  • Flag any rep scoring < 3.0/5; trigger 1:1 coaching slot.
  • Update cohort-tracking sheet with L3 score per rep.

Manager Slack template

Hi [manager],

[rep] just finished the Sales Kickoff workshop. Their 3 commitments for the next 3 calls:

1. [trigger] → [literal sentence]
2. [trigger] → [literal sentence]
3. [trigger] → [literal sentence]

Suggested 1:1 framing this week:
- Open with: "show me your commitment card"
- Ask: "of the 3, which one have you tried?"
- Close: "which one will you try this week?"

I'll pull one of [rep]'s call recordings at day 14 and we'll score it against the observer rubric. Let me know if you want to be in that review.

— [facilitator]

60-min compressed variant (when leadership steals 30 min)

Cut order — preserves L3 bridge

  • Block 1 · skip — open with the agenda + the confidence-1-to-5 Zoom poll only (60 s).
  • Block 2 · compress to 12 min — single round of pair drill, no role switch.
  • Block 3 · compress to 18 min — single round of trio role-play, no envelope-B.
  • Block 4 · skip — name the rule (acknowledge → opener → question) in 90 s during the Block 3 debrief.
  • Block 5 · keep at 15 min — commitment cards + photograph + L1 micro-pulse. Never cut Block 5. Without it, the workshop is theatre.

Total: 1 + 12 + 18 + 1.5 + 15 + 3 wrap = ~50 min, leaves 10 min buffer for setup / overflow. Trade-off: lose framework-debrief depth (Block 4) and the second harder scenario (envelope B). L3 bridge intact.

Scenario card seeds (envelopes A + B)

  • Envelope A (Round 1): María (your existing SCORM persona) has just said "I don't know who spent what — I had to fire someone for expense fraud last year". The rep has 3 minutes to land an acknowledge → opener → question.
  • Envelope B (Round 2, harder): María has been pitched two competing fintech cards in the last 30 days. She opens cold with "I've heard this pitch already — why are you any different?" The rep must avoid the trap of differentiating on features.
