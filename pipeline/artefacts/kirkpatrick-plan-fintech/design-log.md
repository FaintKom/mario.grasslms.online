## References pulled

- **Donald Kirkpatrick + Jim & Wendy Kirkpatrick — *New World Kirkpatrick Model* (2016)** — reverse-design from L4. Required drivers, critical behaviours, leading indicators.
- **Will Thalheimer — *Performance-Focused Smile Sheets* (2016)** — predictive L1 items, not happy-sheet items.

## 1 · Analyse

**Audience for the plan.** L&D / Enablement leadership reading this plan to decide whether to fund the intervention. Plus the stakeholders who would own each measurement.

**Performance context.** Plan is the document everybody signs off on at project kickoff and references at every checkpoint. Must be defensible against "why are we doing this?" pushback.

**Constraints.** ≤2 pages printed. Self-contained (a reader who hasn't seen the SCORM module or facilitator guide can still understand the measurement story). DS-token consistency with the rest of the portfolio.

**Prior-art scan.** Most Kirkpatrick plans in the wild get to L2 (quiz pass rate) and stop. The plan that converts is the one that shows L3 + L4 with named instruments, owners, and cadence — that's what gets funded.

Applied verbatim from New World Kirkpatrick model:
- Decide L4 first; everything hangs off it.
- L3 = critical behaviours + required drivers.
- L2 = learning + confidence + commitment.
- L1 = reaction (engagement + relevance).

### 2e · Modern LXD methods applied

- **New World Kirkpatrick required-drivers** — Block 5 commitment cards, manager 1:1 review, week-14 call recording = the drivers that move L2 to L3.
- **Brinkerhoff Success Case Method** — flagged as the right secondary method at small cohort N.
- **Thalheimer predictive L1** — three-question micro-pulse, not 7-question smile sheet.
- **Performance consulting owner table** — explicit who-owns-what.

## 3 · Develop — build log

- **Decision: one combined plan, not two.** SCORM + workshop is one intervention from the learner's perspective. Splitting them double-counts L3 / L4 evidence.
- **Decision: name role owners (Enablement Lead, RevOps, Sales Manager) rather than individuals.** Plan stays valid through staffing changes.
- **Decision: include cost-benefit at rough order of magnitude.** Reads as serious about value, not just measurement vanity.
- **Decision: cadence per level.** L1 = end of intervention; L2 = quiz attempt + 7 d + 30 d; L3 = day-14 call recording; L4 = quarterly cohort rollup.
- **Decision: explicit risks section.** What if the data isn't there? What if L4 moves but it's due to something else? Stating these up front converts the plan from theatre to honest design.

## 6 · Reflect

**What worked**
- Owner column forces the conversation off "we'll measure it" onto "who's looking at the data".
- Cadence column prevents the L3 / L4 metrics from being decided once and never tracked.
- Risks section reads as honest, not defensive.

**What broke / surprised**
- First draft had Brinkerhoff Success Case Method as primary L3 instrument. Pulled — too qualitative for the funding conversation. Moved to "secondary method when N < 10".

**What I would change next iteration**
- Add a one-page summary card on top for execs who won't read past page 1.
- Add an L0 (engagement) row for completion + dwell-time signals.

**Open questions for stakeholder**
- Is the current SMB ramp baseline measured? If no, that's the first metric to instrument before this plan can produce L4 evidence.
- Is there budget for call-recording analytics (Gong / Modjo)? Plan assumes manual rubric otherwise.
