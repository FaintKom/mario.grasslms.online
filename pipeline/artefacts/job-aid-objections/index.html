Top-7 SMB objections

Use during the call. The acknowledge is half the work — the substance follows.

Apply to every row Acknowledge prefix: "I hear you. [restate what they just said in your own words]." Then deliver the respond. Then ask back. Never skip the acknowledge.
Trust Switch cost Tool / integration Reliability Cost / setup / approval
Buyer says
"I don't trust new fintechs with my money."
They actually mean
They saw a fintech collapse last year. They are not wrong to worry — they want a regulator name to anchor to.
Acknowledge
"I hear you — trust matters more than features here."
Respond (literal)
"Funds are held at a regulated tier-1 partner bank, segregated from our balance sheet. Here's the regulator page and our latest audit."
Ask back
"What would you need to see before you'd feel comfortable moving your business?"
Buyer says
"We already have a corporate Visa from our bank."
They actually mean
They don't want disruption — switching costs are real.
Acknowledge
"I hear you — switching banks is a hassle nobody wants."
Respond (literal)
"You don't have to switch. Our card sits on top of your existing accounts — keep the bank, gain the controls. We're additive, not a replacement."
Ask back
"Who in the company would benefit most from per-card limits?"
Buyer says
"Our accountant won't deal with another tool."
They actually mean
They've had bad integrations before, or accountant has veto power.
Acknowledge
"I hear you — accountants are picky for good reason."
Respond (literal)
"We export to QuickBooks / Xero with categories pre-coded. Your accountant gets cleaner data, not more work."
Ask back
"Want me to run a 10-min call with your accountant so they can ask directly?"
Buyer says
"What if your service goes down on payday?"
They actually mean
They have been burned by a SaaS outage on a bad day. They want to hear "we know, here's our fallback".
Acknowledge
"I hear you — payday is sacred, you can't have a wobble there."
Respond (literal)
"99.99 % uptime over the last 12 months, status page is public. We explicitly recommend keeping one bank card as backup — we tell every customer that on day one."
Ask back
"How would you want to be notified if we did have an issue?"
Buyer says
"How much does this cost vs my free bank card?"
They actually mean
Comparing on price alone, missing the savings + time recovered.
Acknowledge
"I hear you — every euro counts at your size."
Respond (literal)
"Free up to X transactions a month. The math usually beats a bank card once you factor FX savings + bookkeeping time. Want me to run your last month against our pricing?"
Ask back
"What's your monthly FX volume right now?"
Buyer says
"How long to get my team onboarded?"
They actually mean
Heard horror stories about IT projects. Time-poor.
Acknowledge
"I hear you — onboarding usually eats more time than promised."
Respond (literal)
"Average SMB gets the first card issued within 24 hours and the full team set up in under a week. Self-serve KYC, no IT integration required."
Ask back
"What does your week look like — better to start now, or after month-end?"
Buyer says
"I need to ask my partner / accountant first."
They actually mean
Polite no, or genuine consultation. Stay warm either way.
Acknowledge
"I hear you — that's the right call, not a decision to make alone."
Respond (literal)
"Let's get them on a 15-min call so they can ask the hard questions directly. If timing is tight today, I'll send a 1-pager you can forward — but the call beats the read every time."
Ask back
"Which of the two would they prefer — async or live?"
