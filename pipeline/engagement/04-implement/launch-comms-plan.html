2 · Sponsor email · from K.M. to CRO + sales-leadership

Send: Monday 22 June 2026 · 08:30 local · email + cross-post to #sales-leadership Slack From: K.M. (VP Sales Enablement) To: CRO · Heads of SDR (incl. A.S.) · J.V. (RevOps) · D.R. (People) Cc: Designer · LMS admin Subject: SDR onboarding v2 — cohort 1 starts 1 July. What I'm asking of you.

Team,

Cohort 1 of the new SDR onboarding program starts Tuesday 1 July. 10–15 reps. Every 4 weeks after that, another cohort.

Here's the deal I made with the CRO, restated so it's in writing for all of us:

  • Mean ramp from 95 days to 71 days in 6 months. −25 %. This is the public number.
  • Day-120 retention from 83 % to ≥92 %. Stops the bottom-quartile churn that costs us ~€90K per lost rep.

If we hit it, we hit it together. If we miss it, I'll tell the CRO whether it was the program, the leads, or the comp plan — honestly. I'm not interested in a vendor-style "training was great, results unclear" story.

What's in the program: six 10-minute SCORM modules over weeks 1–4 of a new hire's ramp, built around the three keystone moves A.S.'s top reps already use — diagnostic opener, objection acknowledge, calendar-closed next step. M.G. and L.D. helped shape it. J.T. and M.K. helped pressure-test the manager side.

What I'm asking from you (sales leadership):

  1. Block 60 minutes in your manager's calendar twice. Once in week 2 (15 July) and once in week 6 (12 August). These are rubric calibration sessions. A.S. is sending the invites today.
  2. Defend the ≤10-min-per-module promise. If a pod manager wants to bolt on a 90-minute "let me walk you through it" session, push back. The whole design assumes between-call windows. The last vendor built a 90-minute slide deck no one finished. We don't repeat that.
  3. No leaderboards. Exit interviews told us the Monday-morning bottom-3 Slack post drove churn at month 4. We're not gamifying ramp. We're measuring it. The dashboard J.V. is wiring is for management visibility, not for shaming reps in public.

What I'm not asking from you: I am not asking you to retrain your managers. I am asking you to give them a shared rubric (3 rows, one A4 page) and two calibration sessions. That's the floor.

Two things I want to be honest about up-front:

  • Lead quality. Marketing-sourced inbound close-rate is down 12 → 8 %. I'm working that with the CMO. If we miss the 25 % ramp number, lead quality might be part of why. I'll tell the CRO that, plainly.
  • Comp plan. Our SPIF still rewards closed-won regardless of source, which pulls new reps onto stale leads in month 2–4. J.V. has comp redesign on the Q3 roadmap. Surfaced, not solved by this program.

Dashboard wires up nightly via J.V. starting cohort week 1. Designer is on-call for the first two cohorts; after that the program lives in-house with the LMS admin + A.S.'s team.

Thanks for the runway.

K.M.

3 · Manager pre-read · to the 9 pod managers

Send: Tuesday 17 June 2026 · 09:00 local · email + Slack DM From: A.S. (Head of SDR Org), forwarding from K.M. To: J.T. (UK-Manchester) · H.M. (UK-London A) · F.O. (UK-London B) · D.W. (UK-Belfast) · M.K. (DE-Berlin) · S.B. (DE-Munich) · I.C. (ES-Barcelona) · T.V. (NL-Amsterdam) · G.R. (IT-Milan) Subject: Two 60-min sessions on your calendar · what they're for · what I need from you first

Hi everyone,

You'll see two 60-minute holds appear on your calendar today: Tuesday 15 July at 14:00 UK (week 2 of cohort 1) and Tuesday 12 August at 14:00 UK (week 6). They are not status meetings. They are rubric calibration sessions. Here's what they are and what I need from you between now and then.

The 5-minute version of the program

Cohort 1 reps will work through 6 short modules (≤10 min each) across their first 4 weeks. The modules teach three behavioural moves that, on the Gong data, separate top-decile from bottom-quartile reps:

Move What it is What it sounds like
M1 · Diagnostic opener First 60 s of every cold call, one specific question about their business that exposes which pain to pitch against "I noticed you opened a second location in the last 6 months — who's approving expense reports across both stores now?"
M2 · Objection acknowledge When the buyer pushes back, repeat the objection in their own words before responding — then wait Buyer: "We already have a Visa." Rep: "So you've got the card-issuing side sorted." [5-second pause]
M3 · Calendar-closed next step Before hanging up, a calendar invite goes out. Not "I'll follow up." A booked slot. "What's the best 20 minutes next week to walk you through the SMB version? I've got Thursday 10 or Friday 14."

Top reps do all three. Bottom-quartile reps invert all three. That's the gap we're closing.

The rubric

You'll get one A4 page. Three rows (M1 / M2 / M3). Each row is scored 1–5 with three anchored descriptors (what a 1, 3, and 5 sound like). That's the whole document. J.T. — your existing rubric was the seed; thank you. M.K. — yours was the second reference point.

The ask: during weeks 3–6, score 4 calls per rep per pod per month using the rubric. That's it. Not 12. Not 20. Four. The number is calibrated so it fits inside an existing weekly 1:1 + the existing Thursday floor-walk that the higher-cadence pods already do.

What we're not doing

  • Not retraining you. K.M. was clear with me on this: "Don't retrain managers. Give them a shared rubric and two calibration sessions. That's enough." We are calibrating, not training.
  • Not building a leaderboard. Exit data flagged the Monday-morning bottom-3 Slack post as a churn driver. Rubric scores go to you, your reps, and the program dashboard (for trend), not to a public board.
  • Not asking you to be a critic. The rubric is framed as a self-coaching tool for your reps — they should see their score, see the anchor they fell short of, and self-correct. Your job in 1:1 is to disagree with their self-score when you think they're being too hard or too easy on themselves.

What I need from you before 15 July

  1. Read the rubric (attached, 1 page). 5 minutes.
  2. Score one anchor call I'm sending tomorrow (a Gong link to GC-15 from O.B., Manchester, mid-band). 10 minutes. Bring your score to the 15 July session — we'll compare across the 9 of you and that's where calibration starts.
  3. Book the 4 rep-call-review slots for weeks 3–6 in your own calendar. Same shape as your existing 1:1, just with the rubric open. J.T. — you basically already do this. D.W. — this will be new for Belfast pod, and that's the point.

Honest about the hard parts

  • If you have not done a structured call review in 5 weeks (Belfast, hi D.W.), the first calibration session will feel uncomfortable. Show up anyway. That's where the gap closes.
  • Spanish + German pods: the v1 rubric is in English only. I.C. and M.K. — if a rep brings a Spanish or German call, score it on the English rubric anchors and we'll flag any ambiguity for v2.
  • Cross-pod scoring variance is the point of the week-6 session. If we all walk in scoring the same call the same way, we've over-anchored. We expect a 1-point spread per row at week 6. We're trying to get to ≤1-point variance, not 0.

Reply on Slack if any of this is unclear. The 15 July session is the one that matters most — if you can only make one, make that one.

Thanks, A.S. (forwarded from K.M.)

4 · Cohort-1 welcome message · Slack DM to enrolled SDRs

Send: Tuesday 30 June 2026 · 16:00 local (rep's local time) · Slack DM, individually addressed From: A.S., drafted by designer To: Each cohort-1 SDR (10–15 personal DMs) Subject: [none — DM]

Hey [first name] —

Welcome to cohort 1. Quick note before tomorrow.

What you'll get: - 6 modules. Each one ≤10 minutes. You do them between calls, not in 90-minute training blocks. - A simulated workstation inside the module — Outreach, Gong, Salesforce, the calendar, your phone — so you're practising the move where you'll actually do the move, not on a slide. - Two 60-minute calibration sessions with your pod manager (Tuesday 15 July and Tuesday 12 August). They'll already be on your calendar.

What you won't get: - A leaderboard. No public ranking. We measure ramp; we don't shame. - A 90-minute slide deck. The last version of this program was one, and it didn't work. - A script to memorise. The top reps don't follow a script — they hit three moves. The program teaches the moves, not the words.

What we'll ask you for, in return: - Honest 30-second feedback after every module. One question: "I could use this on a call today, 1–5." Plus the move you're most likely to forget. We read every one. - A +7-day mini-quiz — 3 questions per module, takes 2 minutes, retrieval not recall. If you fail it, the module is wrong, not you. - Show up to the calibration sessions. They're where the rubric your manager scores you on gets built in front of you.

Module 1 unlocks tomorrow at 09:00 your time. You'll get an email from Sana.

Honest about the hard part: you will get hung up on tomorrow. Our exit-interview data shows roughly 70 % of cold dials end in a hangup within the first 10 seconds — that pattern holds even for top-decile reps like M.G. (Manchester). That's not a sign you're failing. It's the job. The program is about what you do on the ~30 % of calls that don't get hung up on.

Ping me on Slack if anything snags.

A.S.
