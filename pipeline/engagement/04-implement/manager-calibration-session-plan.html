Why this artefact: K.M. §8 verbatim — "I want managers to walk out of the week-2 calibration session with a rubric they can use on 4 calls per rep per month. Not a 12-page document — a single sheet with 3 rows." Brief §10.8 — the calibrated 3-row rubric is the single most-requested artefact from the manager side. This artefact turns that ask into two run-of-show 60-minute sessions. Framework anchoring: criterion-referenced rubric calibration (Wiggins) + Dirksen environment-gap fix (manager-cadence variance is the environment, brief §9 A.S. pod-level cadence data).

Manager calibration session plans · week 2 + week 6

Audience: 9 pod managers (UK-Manchester · UK-London A · UK-London B · UK-Belfast · DE-Berlin · DE-Munich · ES-Barcelona · NL-Amsterdam · IT-Milan) + A.S. (facilitator) + K.M. (observer) + designer (rubric holder, note-taker).

Sessions: 1. Week-2 calibration · Tuesday 15 July 2026 · 14:00–15:00 UK · hybrid (Manchester room + Zoom for remote pods). 2. Week-6 calibration · Tuesday 12 August 2026 · 14:00–15:00 UK · hybrid.

Framing rule (K.M. verbatim, §8): "Don't try to retrain managers. Give them a shared rubric and two calibration sessions. That's enough." No didactic content. Every minute either scores a call or surfaces variance.

1 · Week-2 session · Tuesday 15 July 2026

1.1 · Outcomes (falsifiable, measured in-session)

# Outcome How we know we got there
O1 Every manager has scored the same anchor call (GC-15, O.B., Manchester, mid-band) using the 3-row rubric 9/9 score cards visible on a shared whiteboard (or Miro) by the 30-min mark
O2 Inter-rater variance ≤1 scale point per row across the 9 managers Calculated live: max(score) − min(score) per row ≤1
O3 Every manager has booked 4 call-review sessions per rep in their pod for weeks 3–6 of cohort 1 All 9 managers show their calendar in the last 10 min; bookings visible

1.2 · Pre-session asks (sent in manager pre-read, Day -14)

  • Read the rubric (1 A4 page).
  • Score the anchor call GC-15 individually before the session. Bring the score sheet (paper or screenshot).
  • Bring own pod's call-review cadence template — pods that don't have one bring their Outlook calendar.

1.3 · Materials

  • The 3-row rubric, printed for room attendees and shared as PDF in Slack for remote.
  • Anchor call GC-15 (Gong link, pre-loaded).
  • Shared Miro board (or physical whiteboard) with a 3-row × 9-column grid (rows = M1/M2/M3; columns = 9 managers).
  • Stopwatch for the 5-second-silence drill in segment 30–50 min (see below).
  • A4 print-outs of the rubric anchors per row, large-font, on the wall.

1.4 · Run-of-show (60 min)

0–10 min · Open + ground rules

Facilitator: A.S.

Time What happens Facilitator prompt
0–2 min A.S. opens. Names the public number (−25 % ramp, ≥92 % retention). Names what this session is not (a manager training). "This is 60 minutes about calibration, not training. By the end of it, the 9 of us are going to score the same call within 1 point of each other on each row. That's the bar."
2–5 min K.M. (observer) names sponsor commitment in one sentence. Then steps back. K.M.: "I committed −25 % ramp to the CRO. If this rubric lands in 9 pods, we hit it. If it doesn't, we don't. That's why I'm here today and not in the room next door."
5–10 min Designer reads the rubric out loud — row by row, anchor by anchor. Slow. Names J.T.'s and M.K.'s contribution explicitly. Designer: "Row 1 — M1, the diagnostic opener. A 1 sounds like this: [reads anchor]. A 3 sounds like this: [reads]. A 5 sounds like this: [reads]. Questions on this row only."

Materials in use: rubric PDF on screen, A4 anchors on the wall.

10–30 min · Score the anchor call (silently first, then surface)

Facilitator: A.S.

Time What happens Facilitator prompt
10–11 min A.S. confirms everyone has the call pre-scored. Anyone who didn't pre-score uses these next 12 minutes to score it. "Anyone who didn't pre-score, head down for 12 minutes. The rest of us are about to write our scores up."
11–22 min Each manager writes their three scores on a sticky note (or in Miro). Names hidden until reveal. No discussion yet. "Don't discuss yet. We want the variance to be honest, not socially adjusted."
22–25 min Reveal. All 9 score sets go up on the 3×9 grid simultaneously. A.S. flips the grid. Pause. Read the room before talking.
25–30 min Calculate variance per row. Per row, identify the max − min spread. A.S.: "Row M1: spread is X points. Row M2: spread is Y. Row M3: spread is Z. Where's the disagreement biggest?"

Variance scoring rule: if all three rows are at ≤1 point spread, skip the variance-resolution block (30–50 min) and use the extra time to expand the call-review booking exercise. This rule is the planned upside.

30–50 min · Surface and resolve the highest-variance row

Facilitator: A.S. with designer support.

The point of this segment is not to teach the right answer. It's to surface why the spread is what it is — usually because two managers are interpreting the same anchor differently. That's where the rubric ambiguity log gets seeded.

Time What happens Facilitator prompt
30–35 min Pick the highest-variance row. Each manager who gave the extreme score (highest and lowest) explains their reasoning in 60 seconds. A.S. (to the manager who gave a 5): "Walk us through why this was a 5 for you. What anchor did you hit?" Then to the manager who gave a 2: "Same question. What did you hear that didn't show up for them?"
35–40 min Designer captures language that surfaces ambiguity. Writes verbatim in the rubric ambiguity log. Designer notes: "Manager X said 'the rep paused, which I scored as M2 acknowledge'; manager Y said 'I scored the same pause as a hesitation, not an acknowledge'." → ambiguity in M2 row 5 anchor.
40–45 min Group re-scores the same row in light of the surfaced ambiguity. New scores go up. "Now that we've heard each other, anyone want to change their score? Same 60 seconds, silent."
45–50 min A.S. and designer note the rubric refinement candidates for v1.1. Do not edit the v1 rubric in-session. Edits go through Phase 6 sprint. A.S.: "Rubric ambiguity in M2 anchor 5 — logged for v1.1. We will not change v1 mid-cohort. Cohort 1 keeps using v1 as-is so the data is consistent."

"Stuck pod" recovery plan: if one manager has scored ≥3 points away from the median on every row, that manager is likely calibrating against a different mental model entirely. A.S. takes them aside after the session for a 20-min 1:1, not in front of the group. Brief reason for the carve-out: brief §18.2 honest-about-hard-parts × no public-shaming. The Belfast pod (D.W., newly promoted, no structured call review in 5 weeks, §9) is the most likely candidate for this carve-out.

50–60 min · Book the 4 call-review sessions live

Facilitator: A.S.

Time What happens Facilitator prompt
50–55 min Every manager opens their calendar. Books 4 call-review slots per rep (~30 min each) across weeks 3–6 of cohort 1 (i.e. 22 July – 12 August). Visible on screen. A.S.: "Calendar open. Slot 1: name the day. Slot 2: name the day. Slot 3, slot 4. Done. Next manager."
55–58 min Each manager states one specific thing they will use the rubric for on their next call review (not in general). "Concrete commitment. Not 'I will use the rubric.' Try: 'On Tuesday with H.K., I will score his next two opening 60 seconds against the M1 row before we discuss anything else.'"
58–60 min A.S. closes. K.M. closes with a one-line thanks. Next session date confirmed (12 August). A.S.: "Same room, same time, 12 August. Bring 3 sample calls from your own pod. We'll calibrate across pods next time."

1.5 · Post-session artefacts (designer produces within 24 h)

  • Inter-rater variance summary table (3 rows × variance numbers) appended to 00-project-management/critic-log.md.
  • Rubric ambiguity log v0.1 (input to Phase 6 sprint 6.1 rubric refinement).
  • 9 × 4 = 36 booked call-review slots confirmed in a shared cadence tracker.

2 · Week-6 session · Tuesday 12 August 2026

2.1 · Outcomes (falsifiable, measured in-session)

# Outcome How we know we got there
O1 Managers compare scores on 3 cohort-1 sample calls (1 per move keystone: M1-heavy, M2-heavy, M3-heavy) 9/9 score sheets visible by 30-min mark; spread per row across all 3 calls computed
O2 Rubric ambiguity flagged for v2 refinement, with verbatim language captured Phase 6 sprint 6.1 ambiguity log updated with ≥3 specific anchor refinements
O3 All 9 managers commit (signed in-session) to the ongoing 4-calls-per-rep-per-month cadence past cohort 1 9/9 commitment signatures in the session output doc

2.2 · Pre-session asks (sent in Slack 7 days before)

  • A.S. picks 3 cohort-1 calls from Gong, one per keystone-move emphasis (a call where M1 was the high-leverage move; a call where M2 was; a call where M3 was). Anonymises rep names in the file metadata.
  • Each manager pre-scores all 3 calls. Brings the score sheets.
  • Each manager brings 1 cohort-1 rep's rubric trajectory (4 weeks of scores) to discuss in segment 50–60 min.

2.3 · Materials

  • The same v1 rubric (NOT v1.1 — v1.1 enters cohort 2 only).
  • 3 cohort-1 Gong call links, anonymised.
  • Same 3×9 grid format on Miro, replicated 3 times (once per call).
  • The rubric ambiguity log from week-2 session, projected as starting point.
  • A4 print-out: "the 4-calls-per-rep-per-month commitment" with space for 9 signatures.

2.4 · Run-of-show (60 min)

0–10 min · Reconvene + variance check

Facilitator: A.S.

Time What happens Facilitator prompt
0–3 min A.S. opens. Reports the week-2 inter-rater variance + the 4-week cadence-adoption count. A.S.: "Week-2 variance was X-Y-Z per row. Across the 9 pods, you scored Q calls in the last 4 weeks against the cadence floor of 36. We're at R %."
3–6 min K.M. names what changed in the dashboard since week 2 (L3 adherence trend, no L4 yet). Then steps back. K.M.: "L3 talk-track adherence is at Y % across cohort 1. Bottom-quartile lift is the part I'm watching most."
6–10 min Designer projects the week-2 ambiguity log. Asks: "Has any of this become clearer in 4 weeks of using the rubric? Or muddier?" Designer: "We logged 3 ambiguities at week 2. Show of hands — who still feels stuck on M2 anchor 5?"

10–30 min · Score the 3 cohort-1 sample calls

Facilitator: A.S.

Time What happens Facilitator prompt
10–13 min Confirm all 9 managers pre-scored. Anyone who didn't — 12 minutes to do so, head down. "Same drill as last time. Pre-scores are honest scores."
13–22 min Reveal calls one at a time. 3 minutes per call. Scores go up on the grid. A.S. controls the reveal pacing. "Call 1: scores up. Call 2: scores up. Call 3: scores up."
22–25 min Calculate spread per row per call. 3 rows × 3 calls = 9 spread numbers. Designer fills out a 3×3 spread matrix on Miro.
25–30 min Compare against week-2 spread. Has it converged? A.S.: "Week 2 we were at 1.8 / 2.1 / 1.4. Today we're at A / B / C. Where did we move? Where did we backslide?"

Expected outcome (per K.M. §8): ≤1-point spread per row per call. If achieved, the rubric is calibrated. If not, it's not yet — and the next 20 minutes finds out why.

30–50 min · Cross-pod pattern surfacing

Facilitator: designer with A.S. support. This is the most diagnostic 20 minutes of the entire engagement on the manager side.

Time What happens Facilitator prompt
30–35 min For each row × call combination where spread >1, identify which 2 pods are most divergent. Name the pods, not the managers individually. Designer: "On Call 2, M2 row, UK-London B and DE-Berlin are 3 points apart. Why?"
35–45 min Have the two pod managers each describe their interpretation in 90 seconds. The other 7 listen. A.S.: "UK-London B first. What were you hearing? … OK. DE-Berlin same question."
45–50 min Designer captures the language of disagreement verbatim. This becomes the v2 rubric refinement input. Designer notes verbatim. Asks: "Is this an anchor ambiguity or a pod-context difference (UK vs DE buyer style, per L.D.'s §10.2 input)?"

"Stuck pod" recovery plan v2: if the Belfast pod (D.W.) is still systematically off-median 4 weeks in, the recovery is no longer rubric-side. It's a cadence problem — Belfast may still not be running real weekly call reviews. A.S. flags for a separate 1:1 with D.W. and offers J.T. (UK Pod 1) as an informal mentor for 4 weeks. Not a re-training; a peer-cadence apprenticeship. Brief §9 cadence-variance is the underlying environment gap (Dirksen).

50–60 min · Commitment + close

Facilitator: A.S. with K.M.

Time What happens Facilitator prompt
50–55 min Each manager states their cohort-1 rep's 4-week rubric trajectory in 30 seconds: starting score, current score, the one row they're focusing on. A.S.: "30 seconds each. Don't name the rep — just the trajectory. Go."
55–58 min A.S. circulates the "4-calls-per-rep-per-month" commitment sheet. Each manager signs. Public, on paper. A.S.: "This commits you to the cadence past cohort 1. The rubric is only as good as the cadence. Sign or don't — both are honest."
58–60 min K.M. closes. One line. Designer notes ambiguity log handover to Phase 6 sprint 6.1. K.M.: "Thank you. Designer hands the ambiguity log to me by Friday. Cohort 2 starts with v1.1 rubric."

2.5 · Post-session artefacts (designer produces within 24 h)

  • Updated inter-rater variance log: week-2 numbers + week-6 numbers, per row per call.
  • Rubric v2 ambiguity log (≥3 verbatim language flags + proposed anchor refinement).
  • Signed 4-calls-per-rep-per-month commitment sheet (9/9 signatures expected).
  • Trigger entry in 06-iterate/ for Phase 6 sprint 6.1 (rubric v1.1 → v2 evolution).

3 · Failure modes + recovery (across both sessions)

Failure mode Probability (brief §20) Recovery
<7 / 9 managers attend Medium (D.W. Belfast, possibly S.B. Munich) A.S. runs a 20-min make-up calibration 1:1 within 1 week. Variance contribution still counted retroactively.
Inter-rater variance >1.5 points on one row after week 6 Medium Phase 6 sprint 6.1 prioritises that row's anchor refinement for v1.1. Variance is not a failure — it's a signal.
Inter-rater variance =0 on every row at week 6 Low Suspicious. Suggests over-anchoring / social-conformity. Designer schedules a "stress test" call (deliberately ambiguous) for cohort 2 calibration.
A manager refuses to sign the cadence commitment at week 6 Low A.S. has the 1:1 conversation that day. K.M. is informed but does not intervene directly. Refusal is signal, not insubordination.
K.M. cannot attend either session Low D.R. (Head of People) observes instead. Sessions still run; observer role is bearing-witness, not facilitation.

4 · Why two sessions and not three (or one)

  • One session is not enough — variance reveals itself on the second pass, not the first. (Wiggins on rubric calibration: 2 passes minimum.)
  • Three sessions breaks K.M. §8 constraint: "two calibration sessions. That's enough." Burning a third session adds cost without diagnostic gain at this cohort size.
  • Week-2 + week-6 spacing is deliberate: week 2 anchors the rubric in managers' hands at the moment reps are still pre-solo-call; week 6 surfaces real cohort-1 call data, which is the only time cross-pod pattern variance becomes detectable.
