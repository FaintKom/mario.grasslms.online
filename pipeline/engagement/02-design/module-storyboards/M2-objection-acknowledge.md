## Gagné event timeline · seconds-level pacing

### 0:00–0:30 · Gain attention · trigger scenario

**Screen.** Mini-OS Gong window opens with GC-09-style transcript (mid-band H.K., §11 — argued and lost):

> **Buyer:** *We already have a corporate Visa from our bank.*
> **Rep:** *But our fees are lower — and the FX is better.*
> **Buyer:** *I don't think you understand. We're fine.* `[click — 1:42]`

Muted-red annotation: *"Mid-band rep, call 3 of a tough day. Argued instead of acknowledging. Call ended at 1:42."* (H.K. §10.3 phrasing: *"By call 3 of a tough day I'm just pitching."*)

### 0:30–1:00 · State outcome

> **By the end of these 10 minutes you restate every objection in the buyer's own words — before you respond. You'll be surprised what the buyer says next.**

Brand-voice peer line (§18.2): *"The argument feels natural. It's still wrong. Here's the move."*

### 1:00–1:30 · Recall prior

"Last module you opened with a diagnostic. This module assumes the buyer pushed back. You have one job: re-state, don't argue."

### 1:30–3:00 · Present stimulus + guide · two worked examples (variability built into demonstration)

**Worked example A · M.G. + Maria · GC-01 verbatim · §10.1**

```
[1:14]  Buyer:  We already have a Visa from our bank.

[1:18]  Rep:    So you've got the card-issuing side sorted.      [ArrowsClockwise] [M2]
                                                                  [silence 0:03]

[1:24]  Buyer:  Yeah — but the controls are a mess. We had a $1 200
                charge nobody could explain for 2 weeks last quarter.

[1:42]  Rep:    Got it — visibility is the broken thing, not the
                card.                                              [Question] [M2 follow-up]
                What does your bookkeeper do when a charge shows up
                that nobody recognises?
```

**Worked example B · L.D. + Tom · GC-06 verbatim · §10.2 (DE pattern + Brex displacement Class 4 variant)**

```
[0:48]  Buyer:  We already use Brex.

[0:52]  Rep:    Brex is great if you're a US-based startup with USD-only
                spend.                                              [ArrowsClockwise] [M2 additive]
                We're built for EU multi-currency and we sit on top
                of your existing accounts — additive, not replacement.
                What does your FX exposure look like on this quarter's
                spend?

[1:18]  Buyer:  Honestly... I don't know off the top of my head.

[1:22]  Rep:    90% of founders don't.                              [Question] [M2 follow-up]
                That's the wedge.
```

**Mayer signalling.** M2 line annotated 3 ways per move per worked example. Two examples → 4C/ID variability of demonstration.

### 3:00–5:00 · Elicit performance · completion problem

**Stem.** Buyer (Emma, §12.3): *"Our accountant won't deal with another tool."*

**Rep (best restate, 4 options):**

- A) *Our Xero sync is automated — you won't add work.* — *(distractor: argues against the objection; GC-09 pattern)*
- B) *So you're protecting your accountant's bandwidth.* ✓ *(buyer-back in buyer-words restate; per `l2-quiz-blueprint.md` §4.2)*
- C) *Have you tried showing them our integration page?* — *(distractor: deflects; GC-12 pattern)*
- D) *Most accountants love us once they see it.* — *(distractor: dismisses; failure pattern)*

### 5:00–8:00 · Elicit performance · solo problem

**Buyer profile (variability — third archetype not in either worked example).** **Lukas** (§12.4) — German hospitality, restaurant group owner.

**Mini-OS sequence.**

1. **Pre-dial.** LinkedIn shows Lukas's profile + recent 4th location opening in Hamburg.
2. **Dial.** Phone-dialler opens.
3. **Rep diagnostic (M1 recap).** Rep speaks M1 — Phone-dialler auto-tags `[M1]` if recognised.
4. **Buyer objection.** Lukas: *"I don't trust new fintechs with my money. We had a payroll thing go sideways with one of those startups last year."*
5. **LO-M2.1 classify.** Mini-OS surfaces a 5-card chooser (Visa / Brex / Accountant / Trust / Fees). Rep picks within 3 s.
6. **LO-M2.2 restate.** Rep types or speaks the restatement. Mini-OS event log captures word-token overlap with the buyer's actual words (≥ 2 overlap = anchor 4+).
7. **LO-M2.3 surface.** Rep asks one follow-up question, waits.
8. **Buyer reveals.** *"The thing is — my last bookkeeper actually walked out over the tooling. So if your team can't deal with simple software..."*
9. **End-of-solo.** Event log committed.

**Success criteria (instrumented).**
- LO-M2.1 family classified ≤ 3 s.
- LO-M2.2 ≥ 2 buyer-word tokens in restatement; zero rebuttal language (Mini-OS rebuttal detector).
- LO-M2.3 follow-up open-ended; ≥ 4-s silence post-question.

### 8:00–9:00 · Feedback · self-score + manager-rubric preview

L3 rubric row M2 (`l3-call-rubric.md` row 2). Rep self-scores 1–5; matching anchor highlighted. Bottom card includes the verbatim P.B. §10.4 line about the argumentation reflex — reframed as the failure mode the rep just avoided.

### 9:00–9:30 · Assess · 3-item quiz

From `l2-quiz-blueprint.md` §4.2:

- Item 1 (LO-M2.1): scenario-classification on "we already use Brex" (the §4.2 verbatim item).
- Item 2 (LO-M2.2): worked-example completion on accountant objection (§4.2).
- Item 3 (LO-M2.3): "Lukas just told you he doesn't trust fintechs. After your restate, what's the follow-up question?" Distractors include "have you considered the regulatory backing?" (close, but is M6 deflection, not M2 surface).

### 9:30–10:00 · Enhance retention/transfer

**Key-takeaway card:**

> *"Restate, then wait. The buyer tells you what's actually broken — that's where the pitch begins." — M.G., Manchester pod.*

**+7-day mini-quiz** auto-scheduled. **L1 pulse** fires.
