## Gagné event timeline · seconds-level pacing

### 0:00–0:30 · Gain attention · trigger scenario

**Screen.** Two-person call preview — Emma (§12.3) on screen-share with a second voice line lit up: *"Joining: Anya — Emma's bookkeeper · 2 min into the call."* Caption (muted red): *"You opened the call thinking you were on with Emma. Two minutes in, Anya joined and asked about Xero. Now there are two diagnostics to hold."*

### 0:30–1:00 · State outcome

> **By the end of these 10 minutes you map any stated pain to one of the 4 props in the buyer's own language — and you hold two simultaneous diagnostics on a multi-stakeholder call.**

Brand-voice peer line (§18.2): *"It took M.G. four months to crack pain-to-prop framing on her own. You're getting it in 10 minutes."* (M.G. §10.1 verbatim *"Took me 4 months to figure that out on my own."*)

### 1:00–1:30 · Recall prior

"M1+M2+M3 + archetype are all locked. New variable: a second voice arrives mid-call with a different objection profile. New skill: which prop, framed how."

### 1:30–3:00 · Present stimulus + guide · worked example (multi-voice)

**Screen.** Mini-OS Gong window + 4-prop decision card visible in the sidebar (Rossett & Schafer 2007 job aid).

```
Buyer: Emma (§12.3) + Anya (bookkeeper joining at 2:00) · industrial manufacturing, 78 FTE

[0:00]  Rep:    Hi Emma — quick one. How much time does your
                bookkeeping team spend re-keying supplier receipts
                every month?                                       [Eye] [M1]

[0:14]  Emma:   ...Three to four days. It's the worst part of
                month-end.

[0:32]  Rep:    Got it — month-end is brutal because the workflow
                is manual.                                          [ArrowsClockwise] [M2]
                What does your bookkeeper do when a receipt arrives
                without a coded category?

[0:48]  Emma:   You'll have to ask Anya — she just joined.

[2:04]  Anya:   Hi — I caught the last part. We use Xero, but
                receipts arrive on paper or email and I re-key.

[2:18]  Rep:    Anya, so the bottleneck is the paper-to-Xero leg,
                not the Xero side.                                   [ArrowsClockwise] [M2 second voice]
                What if I told you our receipt-capture pulls the
                supplier name, amount, VAT, AND posts it to Xero
                pre-coded?                                           [Prop4] [LO-PP.2 in buyer language]

[2:46]  Anya:   That would actually save me ~5 hours a week.

[3:10]  Rep:    Emma, Anya — let me book 20 minutes Tuesday 11 with
                both of you to walk Anya through the Xero sync.    [Calendar] [M3 multi-invitee]
                Want me to add it?

[3:24]  Emma:   Yes.
        Anya:   Please.

[Calendar invite "Emma + Anya · FinTechCard Xero sync demo" sent
 with both emails populated · Salesforce Next_Step_Booked__c = TRUE]
```

**Mayer signalling.** Standard 3-cue M-move tagging plus a **second-voice marker** (`Users` Phosphor icon) on every Anya turn — colour and icon distinguish the two stakeholders. The 4-prop decision card on the right is **spatially contiguous** to the prop-line; the prop the rep selects is highlighted on the card the moment the rep speaks it.

### 3:00–5:00 · Elicit performance · D5 drill (part-task replaces completion)

**Drill D5 — Prop-to-objection mapping.** 15 cards. Each card: a stated pain → rep picks prop (1/2/3/4) and a one-sentence framing in §18.1 anti-jargon language (LO-PP.2). Mini-OS evaluates: (a) correct prop · (b) zero internal-jargon tokens (jargon detector flags "spend-control platform", "expense management orchestration" etc.).

**Pass criterion.** ≥ 95 % on 15 cards. Below 95 % triggers re-drill after the +7-day mini-quiz.

### 5:00–8:00 · Elicit performance · solo problem (multi-stakeholder live)

**Buyer profile.** Variant pairing per attempt: *founder + finance* (Tom + a CFO peer) **or** *owner + accountant* (Lukas + an external bookkeeper). Random.

**Mini-OS sequence.**

1. **Pre-dial.** LinkedIn profile with **two faces visible** (e.g. Tom + Sarah, his finance peer).
2. **Dial.** Call opens with first stakeholder only.
3. **LO-PP.3 first diagnostic.** Rep does M1 to first stakeholder; pain surfaced.
4. **Second voice joins.** ~ 0:90 in, second stakeholder joins; intro line auto-played.
5. **LO-PP.3 second diagnostic.** Rep must ask a *second* diagnostic to second stakeholder (without interrupting the first stakeholder's track). Mini-OS event log: `second_diagnostic_asked = true/false`, `interrupted_first_stakeholder = true/false`.
6. **LO-PP.1 + LO-PP.2.** Rep selects the prop matching both pains; frames it in anti-jargon language. Jargon detector live.
7. **LO-PP.4 multi-invitee close.** Rep selects two slots, populates invite with **both stakeholder emails**, sends. Salesforce flag flips.

**Success criteria.**
- LO-PP.1 correct prop selected.
- LO-PP.2 zero jargon tokens.
- LO-PP.3 ≥ 2 diagnostics asked (one per stakeholder), zero interruptions of first stakeholder's track.
- LO-PP.4 invite contains both stakeholder emails; Salesforce flag TRUE before call end.

### 8:00–9:00 · Feedback · self-score across multi-voice rubric

L3 rubric `l3-call-rubric.md` rows M1 + M2 + M3 — **scored twice each** (once per stakeholder for M1 + M2; M3 is multi-invitee single score). Mini-OS surfaces variance between stakeholder-1 and stakeholder-2 scores; if delta > 1 the worked-example second-voice annotation is re-shown for micro-remediation.

### 9:00–9:30 · Assess · 3-item quiz

From `l2-quiz-blueprint.md` §4.5:

- Item 1 (LO-PP.1): scenario — *"Emma (manufacturing ops, 78 FTE) says: 'Our bookkeeper re-keys every supplier receipt.' Best prop to lead with?"* → Prop 4 (§4.5 verbatim).
- Item 2 (LO-PP.2): worked-example completion — pick the anti-jargon framing of Prop 1 (3 distractors are internal jargon variants).
- Item 3 (LO-PP.3): scenario — *"Anya joined mid-call. You asked her a diagnostic. She answers. Then Emma jumps back in mid-sentence. What's the move?"* Correct: *Let Emma finish; restate Anya's response back to her after Emma's turn → preserve both diagnostics.*

### 9:30–10:00 · Enhance retention/transfer

**Key-takeaway card:**

> *"One pain → one prop. Frame it in the buyer's words — not yours. When the second voice joins, ask them a second diagnostic before pitching either of them." — composite of M.G. §10.1 + brand voice §18.1.*

**+7-day mini-quiz** auto-scheduled. **L1 pulse** fires.

## Mini-OS app set used

Full stack. The 4-prop decision card (`Slack` pinned channel) is **forced visible** at start of module — it is the job-aid that this module trains the rep to *use*, not memorise (Rossett & Schafer 2007).

## Accessibility checklist

- [ ] Second-voice arrival announced via `aria-live="assertive"` (it interrupts the conversation cadence — same volume of alert is appropriate).
- [ ] `Users` second-voice icon `aria-label="Second stakeholder joined the call"`.
- [ ] 4-prop decision card is reachable via skip-link; its rows are buttons (not links) for prop selection.
- [ ] Jargon detector flag uses `aria-live="polite"`: *"jargon token detected: <token>"*.
- [ ] Multi-stakeholder rubric matrix has labelled stakeholder columns; each cell is a 1–5 numeric input with `aria-valuemin/max`.
- [ ] Tab order: trigger → outcome → recall → worked example (turn-by-turn) → drill D5 → solo (pre-dial → first voice → second voice → prop → close) → debrief → quiz → retention → L1.
