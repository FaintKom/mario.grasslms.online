## What we know at the end of Phase 1

### The business problem (sponsor's words)

> "Mean ramp time on the SDR org drops from 95 days to under 75. And we stop losing two reps per cohort in the bottom quartile before they pay back what we spent hiring them." — K.M., kickoff call.

**L4 targets agreed:** mean ramp ≤ 71 d (−25 % from 95) · day-120 churn ≤ 8 % (from 17 %).

### The behavioural delta (the spine of the program)

Three observable moves separate top-decile from bottom-quartile SDRs, consistent across 22 Gong calls + 10 SME interviews:

1. **M1 · Diagnostic-first opener.** Top reps open with one diagnostic question in the first 60 s; bottom reps launch into pitch in the first 15 s.
2. **M2 · Acknowledge-then-respond.** Top reps re-state the objection in the buyer's own words before responding; bottom reps argue.
3. **M3 · Calendar-booked close.** Top reps book the next-step calendar slot before hang-up; bottom reps say "I'll follow up."

**These three moves are the spine of the Phase 2 Learning Objectives, the Phase 4 SCORM scenarios, and the Phase 6 L3 measurement rubric.**

### What we cannot fix with training (Mager check)

Three non-training root causes, surfaced and named at kickoff and re-confirmed by SME interviews:

| Cause | Verdict | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Manager 1:1 cadence variance | **Partial — in scope** | Build reinforcement layer (shared rubric + week-2 + week-6 calibration sessions) |
| Marketing lead-quality decline | **Out of scope** | K.M. owns CMO conversation; documented dependency |
| Comp-plan SPIF distortion | **Out of scope** | RevOps-owned; J.V. handles separately |

**K.M. accepted the cap in writing at kickoff:** "The 25 % ramp compression target assumes I get the lead-quality conversation moving on my end. I am not asking you to fix marketing."

### Who the learner is (composite persona)

**Sam Reyes · 27 · SDR · month 3 of ramp · UK pod.** 12 months prior in retail floor sales; no SaaS or fintech B2B background. ~ 60 outbound dials + 8 inbound responses per day. Hung up on within 22 s in ~ 68 % of cold calls. Time per training sitting: 10–15 min between calls; no 90-min sessions possible.

**Three named anti-patterns** the program will not design for Sam: long-form async video, tool-agnostic theory, public-leaderboard gamification.

## Decisions K.M. and A.S. are signing off

By signing below, the sponsor + champion confirm the following decisions stand as the basis for Phase 2:

1. **The three keystone moves (M1 / M2 / M3) are the engagement's behavioural target.** No fourth move added without a written change request.
2. **The L4 targets (ramp ≤ 71 d · churn ≤ 8 %) and the dashboard sources (Salesforce ramp-time + HRIS retention) are agreed.** J.V. (RevOps) is the next signer in Phase 6 for the L4 wiring.
3. **The Mager scope cap is final.** Marketing lead-quality + comp-plan are out of training-side responsibility. If the L4 targets are missed at six months and these causes have not been resolved on the client side, the attribution conversation references this document.
4. **The persona is "Sam Reyes" — bottom-quartile-at-risk in month 3 of ramp.** The program is designed for this persona. Top-decile reps are sources for M1/M2/M3 specs but are not the design target.
5. **Anti-patterns are locked.** No long-form async video, no tool-agnostic theory, no public-leaderboard gamification inside the LMS.
6. **First cohort live 1 July 2026 is the hard date.** Slip past 15 July pushes to September; all phase gates downstream are timed against this.
