**K.M.:** "Fair. Brutal works for me."

## §02 · Business need · Robinson #1 · 7 min

**Me:** "In one sentence — if this program ships and lands, what changes for the business in six months?"

**K.M.:** "Mean ramp time on the SDR org drops from 95 days to under 75. And we stop losing two reps per cohort in the bottom quartile before they pay back what we spent hiring them."

**Me:** "Which existing KPI moves? Pick one number that's already on a dashboard somewhere."

**K.M.:** "Two — they sit on the same dashboard. **Days-to-first-paid-quota** in Salesforce. And **day-120 retention** in the HRIS rollup."

**Me:** "Baseline today, and target at three / six / twelve months?"

**K.M.:** "Today, mean ramp is 95 days, top decile is 52, bottom quartile sits past 120. Day-120 retention on bottom quartile is around 83 % — meaning 17 % churn before they pay back. Target six months in: mean ramp ≤ 71, churn ≤ 8 %. Twelve months: mean ramp ≤ 65, churn ≤ 5 %. The 25 % compression on ramp is the public number I've committed to the CRO."

> *K.M. had baselines in her head, not on a slide. Sign she has been thinking about this seriously, not handing me a wishlist. Good signal for the engagement.*

**Me:** *(branch — they jumped to "we need everyone trained on X" warning trigger triggered earlier; checked anyway)* "Quick check — when you say 'training fixes this' — have you ruled out non-training causes? CRM, lead quality, manager 1:1 cadence, comp?"

**K.M.:** "Lead quality is messy. Marketing-sourced inbound has been getting worse — close-rate on inbound dropped from 12 % to 8 % over the last two quarters. I've told the CMO. Not your problem, but I want you to know I know it's in the picture."

> *This is the surfacing I expected. Filed: **marketing lead quality is partially co-causing the ramp-time problem**. Training can fix the rep-side craft; it cannot fix bad leads. Will document this explicitly in the Mager root-cause section and cap the training-side responsibility in §06 Out of scope.*

## §03 · Performance gap · 10 min · A.S. joined here

**Me:** "Walk me through what your top-decile reps do that the rest don't. Not the abstract competency — the actual moves. What do they say, click, choose, prioritise?"

**A.S.:** "Three things stand out from Gong reviews. **One** — top reps spend the first 60 seconds of every cold call asking one diagnostic question that exposes which pain to pitch against. The rest of them launch into the pitch in the first 15 seconds. **Two** — when the buyer pushes back, top reps re-state the objection back in the buyer's own words before responding. The rest of them argue. **Three** — top reps end every call with a written next step booked in the calendar before they hang up. The rest say 'I'll follow up.'"

**Me:** *(repeat-back)* "So: diagnostic-first opening, objection acknowledgement before substance, calendar-booked next step before hang-up. Three observable moves."

**A.S.:** "Yes. And they sound nothing alike — top reps don't follow a script. But those three moves are always there."

**Me:** "Bottom 25 % — what do they do that the top decile doesn't?"

**A.S.:** "Skip the diagnostic. Argue with objections. End with 'I'll follow up.' Same three, inverted."

**Me:** "If a learner finishes this program, what is the first observable thing they do differently on the job on Monday morning?"

**K.M.:** "They open every cold call with the diagnostic question. That's the keystone behaviour."

> *Strong sign. The keystone behaviour is named, observable, and measurable on Gong. Designs nicely as the Phase 2 highest-priority learning objective and the Phase 6 L3 measurement anchor.*

**Me:** *(Mager root-cause guard — verbatim from script)* "Imagine I drop a perfectly trained employee into the role tomorrow. Would they hit the target — or would the process, tooling, incentives, or manager get in the way? Top 3 non-training causes."

**A.S.:** "Hand on heart? **Manager 1:1 quality varies massively.** Pods exist where managers do 30-min weekly call reviews with rubrics, and pods where the manager hasn't sat in on a call in a month. **Lead quality** — K. mentioned it. **Comp plan** — the SPIFs reward closed-won, which is what we want, but new reps grind on stale leads chasing the SPIF rather than working the day-1 list."

**Me:** *(repeat-back, then frame)* "So three non-training root causes — manager 1:1 cadence inconsistent, lead quality declining, comp plan pulling rookies onto stale opportunities. Training can address rep-side craft. It cannot fix any of those three directly. What we can do is build the manager-side reinforcement layer into the program — week-2 + week-6 calibration sessions, a shared 1:1 rubric. That puts a floor under manager cadence inside the engagement scope. The other two — lead quality and comp — I will surface in the phase-1 summary as findings for you to address separately, and we will cap the training-side responsibility accordingly. Does that contract work?"

**K.M.:** "Yes. The 25 % ramp compression target assumes I get the lead-quality conversation moving on my end. I am not asking you to fix marketing."

> *Verbal commitment to scope discipline. Will write this into the phase-1 summary and have her sign it.*

## §04 · Audience + environment · 7 min

**Me:** "Walk me through the audience."

**A.S.:** "Newly hired SDRs, 3 months in. ~25–32 years old, mixed prior experience — some have done a year of SDR work elsewhere, some are first sales role. Mostly EU-located, English-primary, some Spanish + German. Tools they live in: Salesforce, Outreach for sequencing, Gong for call recording, Slack for team comms, LinkedIn Sales Navigator for prospecting. Headset on most of the day. Mobile devices not used for work."

**Me:** "Time per learner — total and per sitting?"

**A.S.:** "Honest answer? Total — maybe 6–8 hours over the 0–60 day arc. Per sitting — 10–15 minutes max, between calls. No 90-minute training sessions; the floor schedule does not allow."

> *Cohort tolerance ≤ 10 min per module, confirmed. Design constraint locked in for Phase 3.*

**Me:** "Existing tools / LMS we inherit?"

**A.S.:** "Sana LMS. Articulate 360 license. SCORM Cloud for previewing. Gong, Salesforce, Outreach already wired."

**Me:** "Compliance / accessibility / DEI requirements?"

**K.M.:** "WCAG 2.1 AA. GDPR — no PII in training content. EU-multilingual coverage is a nice-to-have, not a must-have for v1 — we can run English-only first cohort and add Spanish + German once the program proves out."
