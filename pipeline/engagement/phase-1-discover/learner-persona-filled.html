03

Three real scenarios · happy / pressure / stuck

where Sam uses what we ship
Scenario A · happy path · Monday morning
8:50 — Sam pulls the day's call list from Outreach (45 accounts in the warm tier). Opens the FinTechCard "diagnostic-first cold-open" job aid on the second monitor, picks the trigger question that fits the persona ("Who's currently approving every expense report?"). Dials the first account at 9:05. Buyer (retail SMB owner) answers; Sam runs the diagnostic, hears "my bookkeeper is drowning in receipts," pivots cleanly to Prop 4 (receipt capture + accounting sync), books a 20-min demo by 9:14. Logs the next-step in Salesforce before the call ends. This is the loop the program is designed to install as default behaviour by week 4.
Scenario B · pressure path · late Wednesday
14:30 — Three days behind weekly meeting quota. Manager pinged Slack at lunch ("you good? talk later?"). Open ticket from CS about a complaint on the German pod's account creeping into Sam's territory. Sales floor is loud; someone's cheering a closed-won. Sam starts cutting corners on personalisation. Uses the program's "Express triage — 15-min mode" decision card to drop research depth and dial harder on the top-15 highest-fit accounts; gets 1 booked discovery in the remaining 90 minutes. Manager 1:1 the next morning runs the call-review rubric on two of Sam's recordings; identifies that the diagnostic was skipped on 1 of 2.
Scenario C · stuck path · first competitive objection
Friday afternoon. First call with a Series-B FinTech founder who interrupts at 8 seconds: "We already use Brex." Sam freezes — the framing for displacement isn't loaded. Old instinct is "but our fees are lower" (argue). Pulls up the program's objection matrix on the second monitor, finds "additive-not-switch" framing, repeats the objection back ("you've got Brex — sounds like you've already invested in getting your card stack working"), then reframes additively ("we sit alongside, not instead of"). Buyer doesn't book the demo but agrees to a 10-min product-fit call next week. This is the L3 measurement moment — did the program's objection drill load the move into recall before the cold call needed it?
04

Top 3 pains · what costs Sam sleep

surfaced repeatedly across 8 SDR interviews
  • Cold-open rejection inside 10 seconds (~ 68 % of dials). Erodes confidence and skews Sam toward pitch-first defensiveness — the exact wrong move per A.S.'s Gong review.
  • "What do I say next?" anxiety on objection-heavy calls. Sam knows the four core props by heart; cannot recall which one to pitch against which objection in real-time. Loads up the call with hesitation pauses.
  • Calendar-booking friction. Sam often says "I'll follow up" because pulling up the calendar mid-call feels rude; loses ~ 40 % of warm-call momentum this way (Gong analysis, 22 calls reviewed).
05

Top 3 gains · what would make Sam's week

paired with the pains above
  • A predictable 90-second window after the opener. Sam wants enough time to ask one good diagnostic and earn the next minute. Currently averages 22 s before rejection.
  • An objection → prop matrix Sam can glance at on call. Not a script; a decision card. Top reps do not use a script — they use a mental model.
  • A way to book the next step that does not feel pushy. The phrasing matters — "What's the best 20 minutes next week to walk you through the SMB version?" outperforms "Can I send you a calendar invite?" 3 : 1 in Gong sample.
06

Anti-patterns · what we will NOT design for Sam

named misreads — coral-bordered so reviewers cannot skip
Anti-pattern A · long-form async video. Sam has 10–15 minute gaps between calls — not 14 uninterrupted minutes. Three SDRs separately said "I open the Articulate module, see the 18-minute estimate, and close it." Microlearning ≤ 10 min or the module gets dropped.
Anti-pattern B · tool-agnostic theory ("understand your buyer"). Sam needs named-trigger, named-prop, named-response cards specific to the FinTechCard SMB pitch. Theory without the worked example reads as L&D-speak and gets ignored — A.S. confirmed this in week 2 interview: "Reps roll their eyes at the abstract stuff. Show them the words to say."
Anti-pattern C · public-leaderboard gamification. A.S. flagged that the existing weekly leaderboard in Slack already demotivates the bottom third; adding badges or visible streaks inside the LMS would amplify the same effect. Use private % completion + manager-1:1 reinforcement instead. Confirmed by the bottom-quartile interview (P.B.): "The leaderboard makes me hide. I don't want training that does that too."

hand-off to Phase 2
