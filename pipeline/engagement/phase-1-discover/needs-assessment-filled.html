02

Business outcome

Robinson · business need
What changes in the business if this works (sponsor's own words): "Mean ramp time on the SDR org drops from 95 days to under 75. And we stop losing two reps per cohort in the bottom quartile before they pay back what we spent hiring them." — K.M., kickoff call.
Measured by which existing KPIs? Two KPIs, same dashboard:

Source: SDR Ramp Cohort dashboard, RevOps-owned, refreshed weekly.
03

Performance gap

Robinson · performance need · Mager root-cause
Current observable behaviour (bottom 25 %): From A.S.'s Gong review of recorded calls, bottom-quartile reps consistently:
  • Skip the diagnostic question; launch into pitch within first 15 seconds of cold call.
  • Argue with objections instead of acknowledging them.
  • End calls with "I'll follow up" rather than a written next step booked in the calendar.
Desired observable behaviour (top-decile pattern): Same three moves, inverted:
  • Keystone behaviour: diagnostic-first cold-call opener — one specific question in first 60 s that exposes which pain to pitch against. This is the L3 measurement anchor.
  • Re-state objection back in buyer's own words before responding.
  • Calendar-booked next step before hang-up.
Top reps "sound nothing alike" — they do not follow a script. The three moves are always present.
Non-training root causes (Mager guard): Three named non-training causes — training cannot fix any of them directly:
  • Manager 1:1 cadence variance. Some pods run weekly 30-min call reviews; others have not sat in on a call in a month. Mitigation in scope: build manager-side reinforcement layer (week-2 + week-6 calibration sessions; shared 1:1 rubric).
  • Marketing lead quality declining. Inbound close-rate dropped 12 % → 8 % over last two quarters. Mitigation: K.M. owns the CMO conversation; surfaced in §08 as explicit out-of-scope.
  • Comp plan misaligned for rookies. SPIFs reward closed-won, pulling new reps onto stale leads chasing the SPIF rather than working the day-1 list. Mitigation: RevOps-owned redesign; out-of-scope.
K.M. accepted the cap in writing: "The 25 % ramp compression target assumes I get the lead-quality conversation moving on my end. I am not asking you to fix marketing."
04

Audience persona

Robinson · learner need
Role / tenure: Newly hired SDRs in days 0–60 of ramp. Age band 25–32. Mixed prior experience — some have a year of SDR work elsewhere, some are first-sales-role.
Prior knowledge + skill level: Variable. Most have generic sales fundamentals (cold-call basics, CRM literacy). Few have product-specific objection-handling or fintech-segment ICP recognition. Gap is craft, not raw skill.
Motivation + biggest pain point: Motivation: hit first commission within 90 days; stay employed past day 120 (current bottom-quartile fails this).
Top pain: getting hung up on within 10 seconds of cold-call opener. Erodes confidence + skews them toward pitch-first (the wrong move). Loops back into bottom-quartile pattern.
Languages + setting: EN primary; ES + DE secondary. EU-located. Headset on most of the day. Mobile devices not used for work. Cohort tolerance ≤ 10 min per module (between-calls gaps; no 90-min training sessions possible).
05

SME inputs

3 must-haves only
Top 3 things the learner must be able to do (per A.S. + 2 top-decile reps interviewed):
  1. Open a cold call with a diagnostic question that surfaces which pain to pitch against — within the first 60 seconds.
  2. Acknowledge an objection in the buyer's own words before delivering a substantive response.
  3. Close every call with a calendar-booked next step (no "I'll follow up").
Most common mistake learners make today: "Argue with the objection" — bottom-quartile reps respond to "we already have a Visa" with "ours has lower fees" instead of acknowledging the buyer's existing relationship + reframing additively. (A.S., Gong review.)
SME name(s) + availability for review: SDR SMEs (10 total interviews scheduled week 2):
  • Top-decile: M.G. (Manchester, 14 mo), L.D. (Berlin, 11 mo)
  • Mid: 4 reps across UK + DE + ES (named in transcript appendix)
  • Bottom-quartile: P.B. (London, struggling at month 4) + 1 recent-exit exit-interview
  • 2 SDR Managers: UK + DE pods
Review availability: A.S. blocks 45-min slots in weeks 2–3; review cycle 48 h turnaround post-interview.
06

Stakeholder inputs

Hopes + fears
Top 3 hopes (what success looks like):
  1. Mean ramp drops below 75 days within 6 months of first cohort going live.
  2. Bottom-quartile churn before day 120 falls under 10 %; ideally under 8 %.
  3. Sales floor adopts the diagnostic-opener language organically — top reps point new hires at the framing rather than at the script.
Top 3 fears (what failure looks like):
  1. "Another Articulate slide-deck no one finishes." (K.M.'s phrase — previous vendor delivered exactly this.)
  2. Managers do not adopt the reinforcement layer; the curriculum lives on the LMS but never makes it into 1:1s.
  3. Six months in, the dashboard hasn't moved — and the lead-quality root cause turns out to be the dominant factor, not training.
07

Constraints

Robinson · learning-environment need
Time per learner available: 6–8 hours total across the 0–60 day arc. Per sitting: 10–15 minutes max, between calls. No 90-min training sessions; sales floor schedule blocks them.
Tooling already in place: LMS: Sana (SCORM 1.2 compatible, xAPI nice-to-have).
Authoring: Articulate 360 (Rise + Storyline) licensed; SCORM Cloud for preview.
Work-stack: Salesforce · Outreach · Gong · Slack · LinkedIn Sales Navigator — all wired, no swaps.
Compliance / DEI / accessibility requirements: WCAG 2.1 AA. GDPR — no PII in training content (use anonymised personas; e.g. "Maria, retail SMB owner"). EU-multilingual coverage (EN + ES + DE) nice-to-have for v1; EN-only for first cohort acceptable.
08

Out of scope

Malamed · scope discipline
What we are NOT solving in this engagement (K.M. co-signed):
  1. Compliance training (KYC, AML, SCA, PSD2, GDPR) — handled by Cornerstone, K.M.'s counterpart owns.
  2. Manager hiring + onboarding — separate engagement; we touch managers only as reinforcement layer.
  3. Mid-market + Enterprise SDR motion — different sales cycle, multi-stakeholder; separate engagement Q4 2026.
  4. Comp-plan redesign — RevOps-owned; we surface findings, do not design.
  5. CRM / tooling swap — Salesforce + Outreach + Gong + Slack are fixed.
  6. Marketing-source lead-quality fix — outside training-side responsibility; documented as a dependency we cannot resolve.
Adjacent gaps flagged for a future engagement:
  • Mid-Market SDR onboarding (Q4 2026).
  • Manager 1:1 coaching skills (the reinforcement layer in this engagement is rubric-led; an actual coaching-skill program is the next layer up).
  • Account Executive ramp (referenced by K.M. as "next year's problem").
