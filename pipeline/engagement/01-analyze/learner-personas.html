Anonymised composites. All initials match brief §§9–10. Where the brief lists a specific Gong call, it is cited; where it does not, the field is marked "not in brief — propose to A.S. via Gong viewer license."

SDR Persona 1 · "Top-decile" · M.G.

Field Value
Tenure 14 months · Manchester (UK Pod 1)
Pod / cadence context High-cadence pod (J.T. manager · weekly 30-min call review with rubric — §9, §10.6)
Gong call IDs GC-01, GC-02, GC-05, GC-07 (4 of 8 top-decile calls in §11 are hers)
Current behaviour pattern (M1 / M2 / M3) M1 ✓ (38–52 s opener); M2 ✓; M3 ✓ on 3/4 (GC-05 partial — "I'll send a calendar invite")
Verbatim quote > "I never start with 'how are you.' I open with one diagnostic that I've picked off LinkedIn or their Companies House filing. … She paused for 4 seconds. Then she told me her bookkeeper was overwhelmed. From there it was a 14-min call." — §10.1

What she needs from training

She does not need training on the moves; she needs the program to codify what she's doing implicitly so it can be taught to others. Her own retrospective gap was supportive information, not procedural:

"They taught me the four product props in week 1. Useful but not what I needed. What I needed was the framing of when to use which prop against which objection. Took me 4 months to figure that out on my own." — §10.1

Risk this persona carries (per §20 risk row 3)

Top-decile articulation gap. M.G. and L.D. cannot teach by demonstration alone — the moves are unconscious. This is the trigger for D-007 (mini-OS workspace simulation) and D-002 (4C/ID whole-task with worked-example fading: the system fades, not a human mentor).

Threshold concept she has crossed

"Ask one good question and then shut up for 5 seconds. Five seconds feels like an hour but it's where the deal happens." — §10.1

This 5-second silence is the threshold (per frameworks-applied.md §19) — surfaced explicitly in Module 1.

Like M.G., she doesn't need the moves. She needs:

Verbatim quote > "I know the three moves but I forget them under pressure. By call 3 of a tough day I'm just pitching." — H.K., §10.3

Sub-pattern · the cadence dependency

O.B. (UK Pod 1 — J.T.'s pod, the highest-cadence pod) says:

"My manager runs call reviews every week with a rubric. I'm getting better fast but I see other pods don't have that and I think that's why they're behind." — §10.3 O.B.

N.W. (DE Pod 2 — bi-weekly cadence):

"My manager has not listened to one of my calls in three weeks. I don't know what I'm doing wrong." — §10.3 N.W.

Design implication. Mid-band ramp is gated by manager cadence. Without the floor K.M. is asking for (§8), training delivered to LMS hits a ceiling at mid-band. This is the single strongest argument for the manager rubric being part of the engagement, not an add-on.

Sub-pattern · the language barrier (ES pod)

R.A.:

"Half my pipeline is Spanish-speaking but the cheat sheets I get are all in English. I translate them in my head live on the call." — §10.3 R.A.

R.A.'s Gong call GC-13 (in Spanish) hits all three moves; GC-14 (also her) misses all three. The variance correlates with whether she had translated her own cheat-sheet that day. In-the-head translation is extraneous cognitive load (per cognitive-load-analysis.md). v1 ships EN; v2 translation is the highest-priority post-launch item.

SDR Persona 4 · "Bottom-quartile (still in role)" · P.B.

Field Value
Tenure 4 months · London (UK Pod 3 — bi-weekly, ad-hoc cadence per §9)
Pod / cadence context F.O. manager · "Last call review was 19 days ago. He's busy." (§10.4)
Gong call IDs GC-19, GC-20, GC-21 — M1 0/3, M2 0/3, M3 0/3. Avg duration 0:20
Current behaviour pattern M1 ✗ (12-second pitch launches), M2 ✗ (argues on fees), M3 ✗ ("follow up") — all three moves inverted
Verbatim quote > "I opened with 'hi, this is P. calling from FinTechCard, do you have a moment to talk about your corporate spending?' She said 'no' and hung up. That was the third one this morning. By the fourth I just started saying 'I know you're busy, but our card is cheaper than your bank's...' which I know is wrong but I don't know what else to say." — §10.4

What P.B. tells us (the design payload from this persona)

Three things:

  1. The gap is not motivation. She is highly motivated: "I want this to work. I left retail for this, this was supposed to be my upgrade. But if I don't see ramp by month 6 I'm going back. Probably to Klarna or one of the other fintechs hiring." (§10.4)
  2. The gap is loaded-move availability. "I want to know the move before I'm on the call — not have to invent it while a CFO is breathing down the line. The product training I got in week 1 was thorough but it didn't load any moves. It loaded knowledge." (§10.4)
  3. Her manager cadence is the second cause. Bi-weekly officially, every 9–14 days in practice. The brief's own §9 cadence table puts her pod in the bottom half.

Phase-2 implications

  • Module 1 opens with a worked example matched to her opening failure mode (the cold-open hang-up pattern).
  • Modules use Renkl/Atkinson fading: she gets the move pre-loaded in worked-example form before any solo problem.
  • The L3 rubric is the single intervention most likely to keep her in role past month 6.

SDR Persona 5 · "Recent exit · UK" · (anonymised; "exit interview")

Field Value
Tenure at exit 4.5 months · UK (§10.5)
Pod / cadence context Unspecified UK pod; the cadence pattern she describes is consistent with §9 UK Pod 3 or Pod 4
Gong call ID GC-22 (3 calls sampled — M1 0/3, M2 0/3, M3 0/3)
Current behaviour pattern at exit All three moves absent on every sampled call
Verbatim quote > "One — no feedback on my calls for the last 6 weeks of my tenure. I genuinely didn't know if I was getting better or worse. Two — the leaderboard in Slack. Every Monday I was bottom 3 and everyone could see it. Three — the cold-call rejection rate. Around 70 % of dials were hangups within 10 seconds. I started dreading mornings." — §10.5

Why this persona is in the set (and what it forbids)

The exit interview is the negative case. It tells us what the program must not do:

  1. It must not allow the no-feedback-for-6-weeks pattern. L3 rubric + week-2 + week-6 calibration close this gap on the engagement side. (D-004)
  2. It must not gamify negative leaderboards. Per frameworks-applied.md §18 (self-determination theory), competence and relatedness damage from public bottom-3 ranking is documented in this interview. Decision: no leaderboard gamification in v1. L1 + L2 + L3 telemetry stays private to the rep + manager + K.M.
  3. It must not rely on osmosis. Her counterfactual: "If my manager had sat in on a call once a week for 30 minutes and told me what to fix, I think I would have made it past month 6." — the manager-side layer is the retention lever.

This persona is also evidence for the €90K-per-churn business case (§4). She is one realised instance of that cost.

Manager Persona 1 · "High-cadence reference manager" · J.T.

Field Value
Pod UK Pod 1 (Manchester)
Tenure / role 3 yrs at FinTechCard, promoted from senior SDR (§9 implies this is the SDR-org pattern)
Cadence pattern Mon 15-min standup · Tues 30-min 1:1 (alternating call review / pipeline) · Thu 30-min floor walk live-listening (§10.6)
Verbatim quote > "I made my own [rubric]. Three rows — opener / objection / close — each scored 1–5 with 3 example anchors per row. Took me 6 months to develop. I'd happily share it with the program." — §10.6

What J.T. is

  • The reference manager. Highest ramp rate in any pod (§9 implies; explicit confirmation pending — propose to A.S. via Gong pod-level rollup).
  • The rubric template. Her self-built rubric is the proven prototype for the L3 instrument (D-004).
  • The cadence template. Mon/Tues/Thu rhythm is what gets adopted across all 9 pods in the calibration sessions.

Why her cadence works (per her own diagnosis)

"Two reasons. The cadence is real — I actually do the call review every week, not just put it on the calendar. And the rubric is shared — reps know what I'm grading them on so they self-correct between calls." — §10.6

Design payload. The rubric must be visible to the rep before the call review, not just used by the manager during it. This is a UX requirement on the Phase 2 rubric design.

Why this persona is real even without direct interview

The brief does not interview the low-cadence managers directly. This is a data gap. Propose to A.S.: 15-min check-in with one low-cadence manager (F.O. or D.W.) during W3 to validate the assumed barriers (workload? rubric availability? coaching skill?). Failing that, the calibration sessions in W17 + W21 surface the diagnosis post-launch.
