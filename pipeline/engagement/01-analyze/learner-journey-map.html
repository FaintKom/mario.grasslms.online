Why this artefact: Maps the Day-0 → Day-120 experience of the median new SDR across activities, emotion, pain, learning need, keystone-move opportunity, and manager touchpoint. Built from the brief §14 current-onboarding table (what they get) crossed with §10 SME interviews (what they feel) — so Phase 2 sequencing knows when M1/M2/M3 are teachable and when the rep is too overloaded to absorb them.

Learner journey map · Day 0 → Day 120

1 · Framing

The journey is the median new SDR — closer to P.B. (§10.4) than to M.G. (§10.1). The brief gives us:

  • The current-onboarding scaffold (§14): six weeks of formal content, then ad-hoc.
  • The rep's lived experience in their own words (§§10.3, 10.4, 10.5).
  • The outcome distribution (§1): mean ramp 95 d · top 52 d · bottom > 120 d.

The map columns are: stage · activities · emotional state (with evidence) · pain points · learning needs · keystone-move opportunity · manager touchpoint.

The map rows are: W1–2 · W3–4 · W5–8 · W9–12 · W13–17 (Day-120 gate).

This is the journey as it exists today. Phase 2 redesigns it; this artefact tells Phase 2 where to intervene.

2 · The map

Top reps' moves are invisible to the new rep — they sound like personality, not method (per §20 risk row 3 articulation gap). Mid-rep N.W. by W4 equivalent: > "My manager has not listened to one of my calls in three weeks. I don't know what I'm doing wrong." — §10.3 Quality of shadow varies by which senior rep (§14). No vocabulary for what the senior is doing. Manager cadence variance kicks in (§14). M1/M2/M3 should be named here. Today they are not. Highest leverage point in the entire journey. Reps are watching live calls — if M1/M2/M3 were named before shadowing, every shadow becomes a worked example. Variable by pod (§9). In J.T.'s pod, this is the start of weekly call review. In P.B.'s pod, "officially weekly, in practice every 9–14 days."
W5–8 Days 29–56 · First solo dials at volume · cohort wrap-up First solo cold calls + first pipeline; cohort wrap-up live session (§14 row 5); ad-hoc 1:1 onwards Dread + reflexive pitch-launch. Bottom-quartile evidence — > "By the fourth I just started saying 'I know you're busy, but our card is cheaper than your bank's...' which I know is wrong but I don't know what else to say." — P.B., §10.4 · And > "Around 70 % of dials were hangups within 10 seconds. I started dreading mornings." — exit interview, §10.5 (a) No move loaded before the dial. (b) High rejection rate normalised. (c) Argument-reflex on objections (P.B.: "I argue. I know I'm not supposed to."). (d) Calendar not in second tab. (a) Pre-loaded diagnostics per ICP archetype. (b) Pre-loaded objection-acknowledgement script. (c) Calendar-close habit. M1 (diagnostic opener) is the highest-priority drill in this window. M2 (acknowledge) opens here too. M3 (calendar-close) introduced but not yet expected. This is the window where manager-cadence variance causes ramp variance. High-cadence pod (O.B., §10.3): "I'm getting better fast." Low-cadence pod (P.B., §10.4): "He's busy."
W9–12 Days 57–84 · The make-or-break window Ad-hoc 1:1; pipeline review; first commission attempts; SPIF chase begins (§17) Plateau or progress · the bimodal split. Top-quartile by W12 has started hitting M1/M2/M3 consistently (per §11 — top decile averages 8:42 min call duration, 28× the bottom). Bottom quartile inverts all three (§11 bottom 0/4 across all moves). The comp SPIF distortion (§17) starts pulling reps onto stale leads. (a) SPIF temptation overrides M3 net-new behaviour (§17). (b) No feedback loop in low-cadence pods. (c) Bottom-quartile rep starts considering exit. > "If I don't see ramp by month 6 I'm going back." — P.B., §10.4 (a) M1/M2/M3 integrated, not as three separate skills but as one configural call motion (§9 A.S.: "Top reps sound nothing alike. But those three moves are always there."). (b) Resilience to comp-SPIF distraction. (c) Calibrated feedback. All three moves should be integrated by end of W12. This is the latest acceptable window. This is the window where the L3 rubric would be in play if it existed. Currently absent in 7 of 9 pods.
W13–17 Days 85–120 · Ramp gate · paid-quota window First paid quota attainment for top-decile (~52 d already passed) and mid-band (~95 d hits here); bottom-quartile still below quota; day-120 retention gate Bifurcation: ramping reps gain compounding confidence; bottom-quartile reps spiral. Exit-interview evidence — > "No feedback on my calls for the last 6 weeks of my tenure. I genuinely didn't know if I was getting better or worse." — §10.5 — describes this exact window. (a) Visible leaderboard bottom-3 ranking (§10.5) damages competence/relatedness — reps exit. (b) No structured retention-conversation cadence. (c) Bottom-quartile churn becomes the €90K event (§4). (a) Feedback that is decision-relevant, not "you're behind" (LTEM tier-3 framing). (b) Pathway visibility — what does the next 30 d look like if I'm not at quota yet? Less about teaching new moves; more about reinforcement + retention conversation. The L3 rubric provides the language for "you're 1 move away" rather than "you're below quota." The L3 rubric is the only honest substrate for the day-120 retention conversation. Without it, the conversation is "are you closing?" — which is the exit-interviewee's described experience.

3 · What this map sends to Phase 2

Insight from the map Phase-2 design implication
W1–2 currently has zero behavioural anchor — it's product + tools Module 1 (M1 Diagnostic Opener) must run in W1, not W3+. The brief's §14 sequence is wrong here; we shift M1 left.
W3–4 shadow window has no vocabulary Before any shadow, rep watches Module 1 worked-example transcripts (Gong-style annotation per D-006). Shadows become named worked examples.
W5–8 is the dread window because no moves are loaded This is the 4C/ID part-task practice window. Drill M1 in mini-OS before W5 solo calls. (D-007)
W9–12 is where SPIF distortion kicks in Module 3 (M3 calendar-close) explicitly addresses the comp-confound in framing, not as a comp-fix but as a motivational scaffold.
W13–17 is where retention is lost without a rubric L3 rubric live in Gong by W16 (per master-plan.md §5 sprint 5.2). Day-120 retention conversation uses rubric language.

4 · The redesigned journey (preview · belongs in Phase 2)

For reference — Phase 2 will detail this. The redesign collapses §14's six weeks of front-loaded knowledge into a configural arc that introduces M1 in W1, M2 in W2, M3 in W3, and integrates by W5.
