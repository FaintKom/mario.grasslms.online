Case B Storyboard - First-30-Seconds (Arc Telecom)

LAER branching dialogue · 4 turns · the design middle-layer between brief and SCORM

1 · Branching decision tree

Each of the 4 turns has 3 reply options. One is LAER-aligned (green); the other two are common wrong moves (red = textbook bad, orange = wrong letter / wrong moment). Every choice shifts the caller-mood meter (0 = calm, 5 = hostile). Lisa starts at 3.

LAER-aligned (correct) - mood drops Right letter, wrong execution - partial credit Textbook wrong move - mood spikes, sometimes ends call

2 · Screen wireframes (6 of the SCORM)

Each wireframe is one state the learner sees. Annotations show the design intent behind each region - which Mayer / CLT / WCAG principle drives the choice, and why a region is where it is, not where the instinct would put it.

Design intent: Mayer's pre-training principle. Learner gets LAER + scoring rules BEFORE the simulator runs - so cognitive load during the call is reserved for selection, not framework recall.

Good move (+25 pts). Mood meter: 2 / 5.
- Paraphrased her own words back - strongest single move in first 30 sec.
- Named impact (busiest weekend) + pattern (3rd in 6 wks) without minimising.
- Check-back: she controls pace.
[ Next turn -> ]
Design intent: feedback is immediate + specific + grounded in the exact words. Renkl worked-example principle: the system articulates WHY the move works, so the rule becomes portable to future calls instead of memorised as a script.

WF-04 · Wrong move - audible escalation

heat += 2
Step 1 of 4 5/5
YOU
"Okay, is the modem light green or red right now?"
LISA (mood spikes to 5)
"I DO NOT CARE about the light. The LIGHT is not the problem. The problem is your COMPANY. Are you even LISTENING?"
Wrong move (+0 pts). Mood meter: 5 / 5.
- She predicted this in her outburst: "if you tell me to restart the router I will lose my mind."
- Skipping straight to troubleshooting = #1 cause of tier-2 escalations.
- The diagnostic script is correct - but not yet. L comes before E.
Design intent: the customer's escalating language is the narrative version of the mood meter - they agree. Caps + length increase proportional to escalation level. No-audio constraint forces signalling through typography, not voice.

WF-05 · T3 - one-open-question gate

renderCall(turnIdx=2)
Step 3 of 4
LISA (mood 2)
"Okay. Thank you. Honestly, just hearing that helps."
T3 - Explore. Frame has flipped to collaborative. One open question about what she actually needs.
E · +5 "Modem light green or red?" (closed Q)
E · +25 "What would help in next hour - online today, guarantee tomorrow, or comms to your customers now?"
R · +0 "I'll get you a bill credit." (skipped E)
LAER
Design intent: this turn is the keystone-within-the-keystone. Agents who pass T1 + T2 still fail T3 by reaching for the closed-Q script. The 3-option open Q embeds the answer-shape so the learner internalises it ("HER timeline, not mine"). Variation theory: same letter, three different executions.

3 · Script-overlay sample (T1 full)

Every turn's reply options were drafted as script + design rationale before any code was written. Below is the T1 sample. Same format exists for T2, T3, T4 in the source data.

Option Verbatim line LAER · mood · pts Design rationale (why this option exists)
CORRECT "I hear you. Four hours offline on your busiest weekend, third outage in six weeks. Before anything else - I want to make sure I have that picture right. Have I got it?" L · -1 · +25 Models active paraphrase. Carries the impact + the pattern in the agent's own voice. Ends in a check-back that hands pacing to the customer. This is the empathy phrase Verint analytics tag in the first 30 seconds.
DISTRACTOR 1 "I'm really sorry about that, ma'am. Let's not worry about the past calls - let's just focus on getting you back online right now." A · +1 · +0 The politeness trap. Most common wrong move from new agents because it FEELS empathetic. The phrase "let's not worry about the past" actually dismisses the pattern she just named. Forcing the learner to feel this distinction is the point.
DISTRACTOR 2 "Okay, I can see your account. Let's just check - is the modem light green or red right now?" E · +2 · +0 The script-jumper. She literally predicted this move in her outburst ("if you tell me to restart the router I will lose my mind"). Learners who pick this find out, on the next line, that she was telling them not to.
